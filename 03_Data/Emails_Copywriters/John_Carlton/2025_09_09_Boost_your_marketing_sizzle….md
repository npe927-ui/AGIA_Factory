 Shockingly good "buzzwords"...Howdy.

Today, let’s talk about slang. 

It’s always been everywhere, and every generation can’t figure out what the heck the next batch of kiddos is saying. 

I know I could sure use a translator at times. 

But the question is…

… should you use any of these slang terms or buzz words in your copy?

This has been an evergreen debate for all writers and marketers for all time.

The answer is…

… yes, but it depends.

You really gotta be clear about the “who” and the “when.”

Sometimes, a sales message cries out for relevance…

… and the right sizzling word or phrase can knock your reader off their feet.

But ALWAYS be careful of Rule #1 for using slang: 

It’s got to be instantly understandable.

If you suspect for even a heartbeat that you’re going over your reader’s head…

… you MUST translate or explain what you’re saying.

I’ve always sprinkled my copy with quite a bit of slang because I aim to write the way people speak.

However, if I suspect a reader won’t understand, I use “quotes” around the word and then restate the same thing in “plain English” (often in parentheses, like this).

Then I can be absolutely positive I’m not losing anyone.

The LAST thing you want from any prospect is a head-shaking moment of confusion.

Why?

Confusion kills sales.

So be conversational, fascinating, keep the reader on the edge of their seat with a harrowing tale…

… but reread it and edit it mercilessly to make sure it’s clear as a bell and that the words flow effortlessly. 

Make sense?
