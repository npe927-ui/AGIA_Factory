When positivity & fluff won’t cut it…Howdy.

Everyday life offers primo insight into the warped human psyche. 

For example: 

So many people HATE selling and swear they would never stoop to such crass behavior. 

But are you in a relationship? 

Then guess what?

You sold your bad self to someone. 

Whether they regret or rejoice in being persuaded is beside the point.

As Gary Halbert liked to say, we all sell. 

All the time.

And as I’ve been saying for years: Good salespeople just lead better lives.

Cuz they get what they want.

Perhaps even more importantly…

… they don’t get conned into what they don’t want.

I can’t teach you how to persuade in a short email. It’s a lifelong study.

But you can start by paying attention to the lessons that are all around you.

Here’s a story from my own warped past that illustrates my point perfectly. 

As kids, my buddies and I used to haunt an old, abandoned dump, far away from adult supervision.

It was basically a little boy’s version of paradise…

… with old cars half-sunk in mud, ancient kitchen appliances…

… and best of all, acres of glassware of all types.

We’d line up those funny looking old bottles and pelt rocks at them from forty paces back.

One of my buddies turned to me and said something oddly profound for a destructive young punk:

"I’ll bet we had more fun shattering those bottles than anyone had making them."

And so it goes.

Humans work hard to build something…

… and then other humans come along and gleefully destroy it. 

It’s a little foible you ALWAYS want to remember if you’re in biz of any kind:

This innate desire to destroy is much stronger than the urge to create.

This is also why any hint of actual "work" in your sales pitch can doom the sale.

The positive side of buying your stuff is probably awesome. That’s why you’re in biz.

But for the prospect, the positive bits are boring.

Consider, instead, making your message more exciting… 

… by telling people what you’re about to help them destroy.

* Obliterate the old "you", and start a new life, with new goals, new attitude, new look.

* Vanquish the old habits that keep you from succeeding. 

* Annihilate the mess that is your current website, and start fresh.

* Ditch that old car, and enjoy the status and self-respect that comes with the new beast rolling your way now…

And so on.

This “destruction” is often an even stronger appeal.

You can have the best product or service imaginable.

But that might NOT the angle to close the sale.

Instead, it just might be the irresistible thought of crushing something that puts the money on the barrelhead.

Yeah, persuasion can be a little twisted, sometimes.

So you’ll fit right in.

Stay frosty,
