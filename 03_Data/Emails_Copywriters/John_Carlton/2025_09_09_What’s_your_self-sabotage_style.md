Two ways you’re seriously blowing it…Howdy.

All smart biz pros know that if you’re trying to sell something…

… the customer automatically thinks of you as an enemy. 

Whether you sell face-to-face, over the phone, or online via sales copy, you’ve got to figure out a way around that. 

And there are two big ways to blow it in any marketing media:

Sales Killing Problem #1:
Not saying ENOUGH to make the sale. 

Which is why I preach endlessly about the importance of LONG copy in marketing. 

Most people despise selling, especially in person…

… but then at least you have the advantage of some visual and auditory clues to see if your prospect is even on the fence yet. 

My friend Jeff Paul used to tell a story about his own training:

He was sticking to the script during his first face-to-face sales appointment… 

… when his gut just insisted that he add a bit more oomph.

After delivering the “standard” pitch — he added this line: 

“Now, go get your check book and a pen.” 

His trainer almost had a coronary… 

… but Jeff GOT the sale.

This story illustrates why long copy works. 

You have to establish credibility, proof, features and benefits, plus lots and lots of urgent reasons why you should buy right now.

Skip the critical stuff, and your prospect doesn’t have enough ammo to say “yes” to your offer.

Sales Killing Problem # 2:
NOT shutting up when you need to. 

This is the opposite problem. 

Instead of delivering to little information, you blab so much you lose the sale because you missed the moment of truth…

… or you bored the prospect to death. 

This is critical: 

Once you have covered all your main points and countered all the objections to the sale… 

… shut up.

* Even if the silence seems DEAFENING.

* Even if every nerve in your body SQUIRMS, and you have to choke back words.

* Even if you think you’re “LOSING” the sale by remaining quiet.

Just shut the hell up.

Here’s why: 

The decision happens inside the prospect’s head. Beyond your control.

No one buys because a smooth talking salesperson or slick sales letter talks them into it. 

If they buy, it will be because your pitch answered their burning questions and obliterated all objections. 

Often, I’ve discovered that out of several dozen bullets I’ve put into a piece… 

… just ONE made the sale with most folks.

So make the best pitch you can.

But murder every single tangent, anecdote, fancy-sounding adjective that does not further that goal. 

In other words…

… STOP when it’s time to stop. 

Selling requires a delicate balance.
