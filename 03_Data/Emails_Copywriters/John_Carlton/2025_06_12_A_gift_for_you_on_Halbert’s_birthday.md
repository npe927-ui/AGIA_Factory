Now go make a fortune…Howdy.

Another June 12.

I will never not think of my pal and mentor Gary Halbert on this day.

And what better way to honor his birthday than by sharing some of his brilliant, moolah raking methods?

First, let’s talk about his infamous "ethical bribe" angle.  

(Real bribes work too, but he was unwilling to do that.) 

So he would create a goodie-crammed bonus package that was pretty much equal to a bribe in value…

… then use it to demolish all reluctance on the prospect’s part.

Back then, we’d also send FedEx packages to hot prospects. 

This was a special delivery system so extravagant at the time, it never occurred to other marketers to even attempt such a tactic.

However, those packages got past the office managers, where "normal" letters, phone calls, or even personal visits wouldn’t succeed.  

We also came up with ideas like sending the letter from a lawyer, making it very obvious that this was something from the lawyer’s office, personally meant for our prospect.  (Even though the actual product had nothing to do with lawyering.)

We’d actually hire a lawyer to "host" the mailing, which made it perfectly legit.  

There were ZERO suggestions anywhere that this was an actual legal matter… 

… but it still got the letter past the gatekeepers.

Succeeding in a crowded niche demands creative thinking and problem solving.

And there is always a way to solve any problem if you think long and hard enough.

As a freelancer trying to get in front of The Dude to solicit jobs, I also started using real detective tactics — "working" the receptionists for the hobbies, birthdays, and other sundry bits of info about their boss (including juicy gossip). 

Intel that any good salesman can use to quickly bond, create an opening, and follow through on.

(This tactic will sound very familiar to anyone wondering why Facebook is collecting so much personal info…)

It’s all about thinking outside the box, of course, while imagining a loaded pistol to my head.

The world is not fair.

It doesn’t always reward the marketer with the best product or the best deal.

Often, personality, gifts and cheap psychology win the day.

Or ethical bribes.

The point is, you’re proving to your brain that something is at least possible.

This completely inverts how people usually think.

Brainstorm every possible way, fair or foul, that it could be done.

Air it all out, even if it’s taking things "too far."
.
Then walk it back to a plan that meets your requirements for not getting cuffed, shot, sued, or tarred-and-feathered.

Just keep mulling it over. 

What else can you do to stand out to the right people, to win over the advocacy of important groups, to slip past obstacles, to make sure you’re playing the game on a higher level than your competition?

There is always a way to overcome an obstacle.  

And often, somewhere’s between the utterly outrageous notions and the dumb-ass get-yourself-killed schemes…

… is the brainstorm that gets-her-done.

This is high-end salesmanship, folks.
