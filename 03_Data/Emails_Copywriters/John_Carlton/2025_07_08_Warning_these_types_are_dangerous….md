Some people are just plain hazardous to your biz and your sanity if you give them too much attention. 

Even worse, those folks can be hard to identify. 

They’re not the blatantly evil or the maliciously stupid, despite what you might think. 

Nah, the real trouble is with “gurus” who genuinely believe that, because they know a LOT about one subject, that means they know almost everything. 

Many of the folks offering you advice are book learned.

They’re very confident, and oddly enticing…

… which makes them even more dangerous. 

It’s hard to resist someone who seems to have the answers to your most vexing problems.

But all that theoretical knowledge ain't the same thing as knowing how things get done (or don't get done) in the hard, cold reality of life.

Making the world work for you gets incredibly more simple when you learn from savvy, experienced mentors.

Keep in mind that most of what makes for great salesmanship seems "wrong" to civilians.

It’ll all seem pretty counterintuitive to you too, at first.

Persuasion in the real world often defies common sense.

And the skills of consistently closing deals (where money exchanges hands) will forever elude anyone who refuses to understand just how weird and whacky your fellow humans actually are.

Experience matters.
