Is littered with this fallacy...Howdy. 

Ever hear about the need to be “passionate” about your business?

As far as advice goes, this is mediocre at best. 

A little passion will help you get, and remain, all fired up with the process of fulfilling your dreams… 

… so you CAN’T WAIT to get to the office every day and stoke the magic that brings in the bucks, fame and happiness.

And hey — if you got that kind of mojo in your system, go for it.

But that image of constant, unrelenting bliss is total nonsense for those of us who live in the real world. 

You’re setting yourself up for disappointment in a BIG way if you fall for it. 

I know a lot of entrepreneurs.

And none of them swoon while going through bills, meeting payrolls, fixing a glitch on their website, or desperately trying to meet crushing deadlines.

They’re not unhappy, either. 

But no one is like the 7 Dwarfs singing merrily as they head for the mines.

Being an entrepreneur means there’s work to be done, every day. 

When you sit back and reflect, you can smile as your heart skips a beat. 

Because you’ve done, and are doing, something that very few people ever pull off.

You’ve created a biz out of thin air, and made it successful. 

You’re the backbone of American industry.

That’s why I get a little pissy with authors and motivational speakers who insist it’s all about the passion, cuz it’s just not. 

I know many multi-millionaire biz owners who get bored out of the mind with their gig.

Oh, they’re intensely invested in and love what they’re doing.

But they still have to face the semi-drudgery of the day-to-day details and the constant movement against resistance that delivers the moolah. 

And that’s the whole point of this post.

If you can bring in the moolah, you can buy yourself time. 

And in that time you can pursue your true joys.

So get cracking.
