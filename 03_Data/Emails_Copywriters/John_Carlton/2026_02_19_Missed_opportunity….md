By a fraction of an inch…Howdy.

During seminars and hot seats, I often ask a simple question:

What’s the number one way to be successful as a biz owner?

Many folks smartly answer “take action.”

Though not the be all and end all, they’re at least on the right wavelength.

They’re just missing the mark by a fraction of an inch.

And this tiny, subtle nuance can make a massive difference in the level of your success.

Let’s back up a minute:

A lot of  people have a deep, stubborn resistance to what successful marketers call “movement.”

This also coincides with the beef I have with MBA programs.

Don’t get me wrong, I love education… 

… but you need to know where theory ends and reality begins.

Otherwise, you’ll get your ass handed to you when you actually start a business that needs to make money.

Masters degree or no masters degree.

(This is why many high school dropouts can still become millionaire business owners…

… while MBA graduates remain broke and buried under a mountain of debt.)

The same thing can happen when studying online courses on marketing and copywriting.

The learning is great. 

But don’t fool yourself that signing up for yet another course is going to make a difference if you have no intention of testing the concepts in the real world. 

Deep thought, brainstorming, theorizing and other brainy pursuits only get you so far. 

That old adage “Success is 1% inspiration and 99% perspiration” is true.

Yes, you need brainstorming and deep thinking in the mix.  

All top marketers regularly engage in brutal no-holds-barred brainstorming when they begin a project…

… because they want to collect all the info, all the problems they have overlooked, all the hidden pitfalls others have experienced, and all the ways to make the project WORK…

… before they roll up their sleeves and get busy.

However, too many rookie marketers do the brainstorming…

… yet never get busy.

Or get busy exhausting themselves on the WRONG actions.

This is what kills would-be entrepreneurs: 

They are flush with stuff to do… 

… but the real important jobs never get done. 

One decent idea, taken all the way to fruition is where sales are made.

In fiction writing circles, the vast majority of folks don’t want to write a book.  

They want to have already written one… and thus enjoy the privileges of being a “real” writer. 

They sign up for workshops.

Maybe they even scribble out a sentence or two. 

In their minds, they’re taking action. 

Then they’re on to the next “big idea” before their original thought sees the light of day.

This story also provides killer insight as to why there aren’t more successful entrepreneurs online.  

Aspiring marketers have the “thinking” part down.  

Great ideas, fabulous long-range plans, oodles of sizzling passion.

But somehow, they just can’t pull the trigger.

Their bugaboo is “action”.

And not just any action, but by taking REAL steps to get their marketing materials and products into REAL customers’ hands. 

No theory, no BS.

Movement gets more done than all the pondering in history. 

Learning is great. 

So learn what you need to do.

And then do it. 

Write that down and stick it on your wall.
