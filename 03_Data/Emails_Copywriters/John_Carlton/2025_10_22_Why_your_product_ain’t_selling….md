If you’ve hung around in my world long enough, you know that few things can kill a sale faster than boring your prospect to tears during a pitch. 

But boredom isn’t the only thing that tanks your conversion rate. 

Few people are aware that you can get your prospect whipped up into a frenzy of excitement and still lose the deal. 

And this all comes down to human psychology 101: 

People HATE change. 

We may crave it, need it, it could be what is best for us, we might even shout from the rooftops that we love it…

… but deep down, everyone fears it.

If what you sell requires any kind of change —then you can expect your reader to resist. 

No matter how good your product is at helping people create better lives…

… often, unconsciously, they are happier in their familiar funk.

This is one of the biggest obstacles you will encounter. 

I consult with clients all the time who have a great product, but can’t make the sale because they are simply flabbergasted when rational prospects hesitate to buy.

So how do you avoid losing the sale, either to boredom or the threat of change?

The key is to avoid taking anyone too far out of their comfort zone too fast. 

Know your obstacles. 

Understand your prospect.

Keep your sales messages exciting yet believable, and don’t go off on a tangent even for a second.

Oh, and do tons of research. 

Never try to sell in the dark.
