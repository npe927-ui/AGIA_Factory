Your happiness & success depends on this knowledge…Howdy.

Age, health, gender, and even income have almost NOTHING to do with whether or not someone is happy or not.  

For example: 

When I first started succeeding as a freelancer, I quickly noticed that there were two types of  biz owners:

1) Those who were having fun… 
2) And those who NEVER had fun, ever. 

The difference?

It was simply how they handled stress.

Not what they KNEW about what was the "right way" stress…  

… but how they DEALT with it.

Anyone can talk a good game. 

But still, most folks "deal" with stress by waiting for it to boil over into crisis-mode…

… then spend their savings and every moment of consciousness left trying to fix what’s broken.

Here’s a much better plan: 

Gather a couple of good tools for your Bag O’ Tricks, and USE them. 

To get you started, here’s what I came up (which has worked nicely for me for 30 years):

SUPER SUCCESSFUL STRESS-MURDERING TACTIC: 

You wanna bust Mr. Stress in the chops?

Write yourself a letter to put aside for 24 hours.

Take the phone off the hook, lock the door, and give yourself a solid hour to do this.

This letter is basically a "Status Report" of your life at this moment.

Lay it all out.  All your troubles, all your faltering plans, all your suspicions about coworkers, all your fears about your health, happiness and future.

I like to use numbered items, so I don’t have to bother with segues between paragraphs or sentences.  

Just lay out one thought, hit "return" on the keyboard and start on the next numbered item.

Don’t limit yourself, in any way.  You’re going to articulate pains that no one else will ever see… so don’t hold back.

When you’ve exhausted all items on your mind, you’ll have two things:

1) A clear "To Do List"
2) And a candid assessment of your state of mind right now.

This exercise is a very cheap and effective psychological trick.

Writing it all down —the bad ideas and the brilliant realizations and the mundane shit that you can’t quite believe you care about — allows your brain to let go.

Kind of like a dog napping near his buried bone. You can relax completely.

During the 24 hour "grace period", you can REALLY unplug because you’re not giving up on what’s bugging you.

You’re just putting it aside for a bit.

Now go have some fun as you allow your unconscious to mull over what you’ve written.

When you get BACK to your Status Report in 24 hours…

… you will suddenly have perspective you couldn’t muster before.

It works for me, anyway.

Cuz what good is being wealthy or successful if you’re not actually having any fun with it?
