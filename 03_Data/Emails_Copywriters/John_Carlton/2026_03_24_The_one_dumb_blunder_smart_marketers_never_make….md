Ever, under any circumstance…Howdy.

One of the first steps to outrageous success is first figuring out why you’re not outrageously successful already. 

Seriously, what’s holding you back? 

Fixing what’s broken is surprisingly the easy part. 

The hardest thing is diagnosing what went sideways in the first place. 

It might come down to a lack of skill or knowledge. No problems there, just get busy filling in those holes. 

Far worse are the personality traits that bury you in the muck. 

* Procrastination
* Lack of confidence
*The failure to finish anything you start

One of the most dangerous by far is false confidence. 

(It’s hard to say what’s worse, that or low self-esteem. Both are success killers.) 

But unchecked belief systems can really make the believer delusional. 

For example:

You can believe — with all your heart and soul — that you’re the baddest ass in the bar… the prettiest girl at the beach… or the savviest hustler on the street.

It’s your privilege to believe anything you want.

Just know that you’ll run into trouble if you expect reality to play along.

Here’s that thing smart marketers never do under any circumstance: 

They don’t rail against what people believe or try to "change their minds" using logic and reason.

Instead, they work with what they got.

Rookie salespeople shrivel away when they see a wall of objections and beliefs that run counter to buying their product…

… whereas a world-class sales pro is eager hear ALL the reasons their prospect was refusing to buy.  

______________________________________________________________________

One of the first lessons my mentors taught me is an old door-to-door salesman tidbit of street-wise psychology. 

Homes with "No Solicitation" signs on the front door sent lesser mortals scurrying off for easier pastures.

But the real pros just laughed (all the way to the bank, that is.)

You know why? 

Because those were the easiest prospects to close. Hands down.

______________________________________________________________________

Savvy marketers and sales geniuses realize that even the most rock-solid psychological "electric fence" of resistance… 

… is just a rickety pile of simple objections. 

By the time a master is done, the prospect is standing there thinking, "Darnnit, this smart person is right. I do want that thing."

______________________________________________________________________

But here's the thing:

Deep down, the prospect thinks it was all their idea to buy, not for a hot second that they got sold. 

Read that above sentence again, because there’s a lot of truth in it.

______________________________________________________________________

This is why I want to teach salesmanship to everyone.

People who understand these concepts lead better lives. 

Not necessarily because they’re better people… 

… but because they are unencumbered with the burden of stupid beliefs.

And, they understand the process of selling that is going on in every store, on every website, in every magazine, on every TV station… and between every set of humans alive — spouses, friends, neighbors, colleagues, enemies, and even strangers.
