Must-have insight for marketers…Howdy.

Smart marketing means being aware of the darker tendencies of human behavior. 

To some extent, we all lay on the charm and tell little lies to manipulate others.

Sometimes you can’t avoid it because it’s a mandatory part of playing nice with others. 

* So you smile and say nice things to loathsome relatives.

* You praise your boss even when you’re fairly sure they’ve just done something mind-bogglingly stupid.

* You needle your buddies until they finally agree to help you move or attend the Big Game despite initially not wanting to.

Here’s a story I’m not sure I’ve told before. 

Halbert — after realizing that doctors were treated with deference at hotels — started checking in as “Dr. Finegold.” 

This was a personality he created just to get past the usual hassle in dealing with hotel staff. 

His innate moral compass meant he never pushed the concept very far…

… but it was stunning how much he could accomplish (like getting meals sent up faster, and phone calls put through instantly) just by letting the staff think he was a doctor. 

It didn’t always work, mind you.

But it was an insight to how easy it can be to manipulate people when you understand human behavior.

Side note: We also experimented with “tips” – and not just the socially acceptable amount. 

These skated closer to bribes…

… greasing the palms of clerks, bouncers, waiters, anyone who could sneak us past the time-wasting nonsense people normally have to endure. 

We even tested dropping names — because we actually knew celebrities who frequented certain joints like Spagos. 

The results are startling.

A real eye-opener if you’ve never engaged in gaming systems before. 

There will always be traces of sociopathology in the people you deal with, live with, and love. 

We’re imperfect beings, and sometimes we take shortcuts to get what we want.

(Like telling the wife you’re gonna be ten minutes late for dinner, when you know damn well it’s gonna be 45 minutes.)

And, if you desire success (in biz or love), you’ve got to get hip to spotting the real monsters from the regular folks. 

It takes effort and practice…

… and you must toe a very ethical and responsible line of action.
