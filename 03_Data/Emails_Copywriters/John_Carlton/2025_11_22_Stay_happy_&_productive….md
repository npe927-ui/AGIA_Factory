Howdy.

If you're not using copious amounts of downtime as a productivity hack, then you're definitely missing out on your full potential.

You're probably also not as happy or as healthy as you should be.

So let's fix that with one of my secret techniques for achieving your lofty goals.

It all starts with treating
yourself more often.

Especially after winding up a big project.

For me, I always rewarded myself with free time...

... as in unplugging completely for the weekend.

It doesn't matter what it is as long as it's meaningful to you.

* A new watch. 

* A vacation. 

* Or just a weekend off.

Just the ACT of rewarding yourself fires up the motivation part of your unconscious brain.

The Puritan Azzhole that still rules most American's brains thinks that work should be its own reward.

Screw that. 

When you move into the upper levels of biz (where the risks are greater and the moolah more lavish)...

... you need every trick in the book to stay in top form.

Finishing up a grueling gig, clearing the desk, and then starting the next grueling gig is the FASTEST path to burnout.

What the hell are you thinking, you're some kind of Marvel superhero?

Humans are NOT machines.

You need to decompress.

So your marching orders are to go shop for a goodie and teach your brain to
associate the end-of-job with fun rewards.

Important: 

The reward cannot be something you'd buy or do anyway. 

It has to be pure excessive nonsense that you would NEVER do for yourself otherwise.

In other words, go find a way to delight your Inner Kid.

Stay frosty,

John
