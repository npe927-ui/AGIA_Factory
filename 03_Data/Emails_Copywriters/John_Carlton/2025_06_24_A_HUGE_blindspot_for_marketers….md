Stop outsmarting yourself already… Howdy. 

Ever wonder if the reason you’re struggling is that you’re just too freaking smart for your own good? 

Now with A.I. in the mix, you're in more danger than ever of outsmarting yourself and losing sales as a result. 

As copywriters and marketers, you are - and should always be - on an never ending mission to increase the brain wattage in your noggin.

Just be aware that the only person you are outsmarting might be yourself.

And never lose sight of the most the most important skill you could ever possess:

The ability to communicate with your audience.

That’s why one of the first things I have to do, to be able to move y'all up a level in salesmanship…

…is knock some of the smart out of you.

In fact, the bigger the IQ…

… the lousier the marketing chops. 

Why?

Two reasons:

* Selling requires communication. 

* Communication requires conversation on the level of your prospect.

And if you use words your reader doesn't understand, or concepts he's vague on, or cool hipster language from a corner of the culture he's clueless about…

… you've just murdered the sale, Bucko.

It's often a BIG blind spot, too.

I mean, all your friends understand you just fine. 

Your colleagues in the gig think you're just a wonderful conversationalist. 

So why shouldn't your market respond the same way?

Well, they don't.

If you have an above-average lump of gray matter sloshing around in your brain pan…

… then half your audience probably isn't following your high-end ramblings.

And no decent marketer wants that on their conscience.

It just ain't profitable.

The old-school rule is: 

Write at a fifth-grade level. (And save the fancier language for your novel in the bottom drawer.)

In any case… write simply. 

(If you're using A.I. for your first draft, tell it to write simply. Then edit it fiercely. You can't rely on the machines for everything, you know.)

Avoid adjectives and bombast altogether.

Strive for a straightforward communication style that matches the conversations already going on in your prospect's mind. 

I call it "just you and me" writing — it's just like you and me sitting across from each other, having a chat.

Aim for maximum persuasion.

Don't bully, plead or lecture.

Just lay out your sales message plainly. 

Stuffed with the benefits, hooks, and credibility that makes your offer a can't-refuse option.

Strive to sound like someone your prospect can trust, and would want to hang out with.

Not the nerdy, over-educated twit far too many entrepreneurs try to emulate.

Bonus tip: 

Add a little salty language, when appropriate.

It gives your message that special "real talk" advantage that readers appreciate.

And for cryin' out loud… 

NEVER, ever make me read anything twice to figure out what the hell you're trying to say.

Be clear. 

Be effective, within the language parameters of plain English.

And be persuasive.

You've got some sales to make here, Brainiac.
