Then don’t make this blunder & blow it all up…Howdy.

Here we are on the cusp of another July 4th celebration, getting ready for the explosions and firecrackers. 

And speaking of blowing things up, that’s exactly what these folks who are thriving in their biz tend to do. 

Becoming complacent is a massive blunder. 

When things are going well, it's tempting to get waaayyyy too comfy. 

You might even start the celebratory fireworks too early.

Just beware: 

When things seem to be back on track… 

... the money's pouring in, clients are happy, the rent is paid…

… you should NEVER stop driving for the next sale or the next client.

It's nice when things are finally going your way. 

But you never know if or when that "sure thing" you're enjoying is gonna blow up.

I've coached countless entrepreneurs, and those were some of the saddest phone calls ever. 

They're stunned but gotta get something new going, fast. 

Cuz the wolf will be at the door soon.

Any number of things can go wrong even when it seems it's all sunshine and roses.

The client got pissed off, died, or was dragged off to the hoosegow by the feds. 

The money stopped coming in, abruptly and rudely.

And there was nothing else in the pipeline.

Cuz, you know, it was all going so well.

There are no "time outs" in a good biz model.

There ARE lots of great vacations, time off, and enjoying the good grooves while they're happening…

… but never take tomorrow for granted.

Part of a great biz plan is to have a plan for when things go rancid. 

A nice pile of "screw you" money set aside for a stormy day helps immensely.

Think about how you want the next few years to play out. 

And about what it's gonna take to fund that adventure, and what will be required of you to make those funds appear.

Especially now, with so much upheaval.

If times are good, enjoy them. 

Just remember that fate is fickle, and a perfect situation can suddenly collapse into chaos.

This is particularly important now, as all the old rules seem to be changing faster than we can blink. 

There’s never a time to be on cruise control in biz. 

Stay frosty,
