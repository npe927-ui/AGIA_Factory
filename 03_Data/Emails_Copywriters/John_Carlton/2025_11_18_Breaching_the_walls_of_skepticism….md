If you can't do this, you can't sell...Howdy.

Cold copy to cold prospects is one of the toughest challenges you’ll ever tackle.

Even warm prospects are more skeptical than ever…

… but someone who has never heard of you takes the cynicism up to the stratosphere.

And their disbelief borders on hostility. 

But here is some good news:

There’s a simple way to breach this wall of disbelief and lack of trust (learned from my long years as a successful copywriter):

Just be “real” with your reader whenever possible.

Now, this doesn’t mean you should tell naughty stories to bank presidents when you’re writing B2B… 

… but you DO need to write in a voice that rings true to your reader. 

(Dirty secret: I happen to have known some bank presidents in my time. They are, sometimes, wild and crazy guys who are just as bored with formal business dealings as you are thinking about them.)

If you’re good, you can sneak into their good graces through a side door no one else is using.

Which is why I’ve adopted a teaching style that is vicious and fun…

… and also shockingly effective with the RIGHT student.

With the wrong student… not so much.

One of my first mandates when pitching my training programs was to discourage the “wrong” people from attending. 

No sense of humor? Stay home. 

Indignant about being taken apart by a mere copywriter? Go get an MBA.

Can’t wait to roll up your sleeves, put your ego on a shelf, and get down and dirty with the reality of what it takes to write world-class advertising?

You’re my man. Or woman. 

Or extraterrestrial. I don’t care.

If you  fill your marketing to the brim with personality (as you should as often as possible)...

… you’re going to offend some people. 

It’s just an unavoidable fact of life.

Try to be everyone’s cup of tea, and you’re no one’s cup of tea.

So the first step in succeeding as a marketer is in knowing who you’re trying to reach.

Then learning the language to reach them.

Learning to craft a killer story will help.

If you missed it, there’s a great crash course in storytelling over at john-carlton.com/bring-your-badass-story-home-to-your-reader

Stay frosty,
