Simple trick to bypass client's stubbornness... Howdy.

Have you ever stopped to think just how freaking stubborn people can be?

I mean, insanely stubborn, to the point of self-sabotage?

I used to ask students to partake in a simple experiment: 

Try getting one of your pals to go see a movie you recommend.

Make sure it’s a movie they would like. And would probably go see on their own without being prodded by you.

Now tell them how much you loved it, and make the suggestion that they just GOTTA go see it themselves.

Then… watch what craziness happens next.

It’s astonishing how often your BFF, upon being told how awesome a movie is, how much they’d like it, and especially how much YOU liked it…

… will now refuse to see it themselves.

You laugh.

But try it.

The stories any salesman can tell you about the roiling stubbornness of prospects are legion.

Even when their own self-interest is at stake.

They just won’t. 

* Won’t buy

* Won’t click

* Won’t pick up the phone

* Won’t walk four seconds to pick up a free sample.

Why?

Just cuz.

This human glitch has been the bane of sales pros, copywriters, and marketers since the dawn of time.

It’s also a core lesson for anyone trying to sell something to someone else (and yes, you’re "selling" that movie to your buddy).

People outright refuse to do things that will make their lives better. 

* Without good reason. 

* Without logical explanation. 

* Without knowing themselves why they’re being so stubborn.

This is what you’re up against when you’re in biz.

Yes, it’s annoying.

But understanding that good salesmanship entails more than telling someone to do something will ultimately change your life.

You gotta master the sneaky skills of real persuasion.

All the latest and greatest in tech can’t help you if you don’t do that. (Case in point, just look at how many tech companies themselves are flailing these days).

Here’s something to ask yourself:

How much money and time have you invested learning about new tech? How much did you spend on the latest cool-as-shit equipment for your biz? A lot, right?

All good stuff. It might make your life easier. But only if you master the fundamentals first.

So here’s another all important question:

How much did you spend sharpening your skills as a sales pro?

If you’re like most of the folks I talk to, you’ve been starving your Inner Salesman — of resources, support, and of knowledge about the basics of human behavior.
