Psst! Here’s the real secret…Howdy.

It happened almost every single day at every seminar or hot seat I’ve ever been a part of. 

At some point, someone would always come up to me and whisper, “so what’s the REAL secret to all killer marketing and copywriting?”

As if I were keeping “the good stuff” from everyone else who paid to attend. 

But the truth is both ridiculously simple and one of the great mysteries of the universe:

Effective marketing is really all about communication http://john-carlton.com/how-to-communicate-with-humans.

There are no instant hacks.

You just have to be able to get your message across succinctly…

… and even more importantly…

… with REAL empathy.

Communication is the ticket. 

NOT clever wordplay. 

NOT schmoozing people. 

It’s so easy… 

… and yet so hard. 

Even creativity can’t get the job done if you don't know how to get into the heads and hearts of your prospect. 

That’s why empathy is the freakin' nuclear bomb of high-end communication.

Lots of marketers can't pull off genuine empathy…

… because it means caring more than just about your prospect’s wallet.

You seriously do give a damn how your reader is feeling.

The effect becomes even more mind-blowing because most folks simply can’t or won’t show empathy. 

The vast majority of your neighbors think, speak and act from inside a cramped echo chamber…

… where their own prejudices, beliefs, notions and cockamamie thoughts completely dominate.

By far the hardest thing I've been trying to teach people over my career…

… is that good salesmanship is a tool.  

Like a hammer. 

A hammer works to pound nails into the foundation of your dream house just as effectively as it can pound holes in the head of your mother-in-law when you finally lose it.

The hammer doesn't care who uses it or why.

This is why empathy is so critical, especially against all the white noise in the marketplace today. 

Ethical and honest businesses NEED to use salesmanship to get their helpful products into customers' hands.
