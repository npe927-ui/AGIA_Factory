Here’s a super quick lesson on intuition, but a really important one.

Thing is… loads of folks rely on gut instinct.  

But when there’s money on the line, this often leads to unmitigated disaster.

As a freelancer, I always told clients: “It’s a mess to guess.”

That little nugget is as true as it ever was.

When I was coaching the SWS, I used to tell members point blank that intuition is just dead wrong.  

After much discussion, it was amended to “untrained intuition is almost always dead wrong.”

What’s the difference between good intuition and bad intuition?

It’s worth studying up on.

When most folks talk about intuition, however, they are really just employing “wishful thinking“. 

They haven’t yet figured out how to access the quasi-unconscious state where info is processed and run through our life’s experience… 

... which is what “intuition” actually is.

Take poker, for example. 

You can have all the “feeling” you want about another player’s hand. 

But if you’re wrong, you can lose your shirt.

Same with writing sales copy or testing out a new marketing funnel.

You might think it’s gonna hit it out of the park.

But if you don’t test results, or go deeper into WHY you felt the way you felt…

… you’ll never train your intuition to recognize an actual winner.

So here is my Grizzled Veteran Advice: 

All rookies should NOT rely on intuition until they’ve proven that those feelings actually are worth relying on.  

That takes time.

To train yourself, study the process in your head as it happens… take notes… keep score.

All top writers go through this gauntlet. 

It’s how they GET good.

Your intuition, left wild and untamed, can get you hurt.

Once trained, vetted and tempered, you will possess a powerful tool for moving through life like a ninja pro.
