Successful marketers have their differences…

That's when it hits me. 

There are some absolutely brilliant minds at this table:

* Technical wizards 

* Stunningly talented thinkers 

* Writers with breathtaking talent 

The kicker is that none of these people were successful due to brain power or cutting edge creativity. 

(Lots of folks have that but never get anywhere).

The real secret is this:

We are ALL misfits in some way or another. 

None of us would fit well in a regular job. 

A few have, at some point, been diagnosed with an attention deficit disorder or a learning disability or even forced into special needs programs at school. 

Our talents always overlooked and underestimated in the mainstream world. 

Like I said, all misfits. 

Maybe not fitting into the tidy boxes the world wants to stuff you into made you feel like a total failure in life.

But not anymore.

Because the opposite is true.

Imagine that all those quirks that made you such a "weirdo" in polite society…

…  are actually signs of underestimated brilliance. 

You have unique experience, knowledge, and information that no one else has.

Maybe you break conventional thinking a little bit, but all great minds have at one point or another. 

And here's another little secret about success in the online world:

Your story and your personality matter as much as the quality of your product and your message. 

Your actual methodology of whatever you offer has almost certainly been created before…

… but NEVER in the unique and fascinating way that only you can present it. 

The big lesson here?

Let your freak flag fly, and stop hiding your light under a bushel.

Being a misfit is a good thing, especially online.
