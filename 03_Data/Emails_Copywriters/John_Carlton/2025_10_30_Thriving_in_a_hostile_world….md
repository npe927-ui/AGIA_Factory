Start by getting rid of crushing anxiety…Howdy.

Do you have a good handle on what causes you to FREAK out?

In other words, what gets you triggered?

Laugh if you want, but we all have them.

I've been studying how people deal with anxiety ever since I was an introverted, stuttering kid…

... trapped in an extroverted world while I was full of social awkwardness.

And there were a stunning array of opportunities to be humiliated.

I didn't ENJOY being jittery.

I just figured it was part of surviving in a hostile world. 

However…

… when I kickstarted my career as an entrepreneur, the anxiety levels ramped up to 11 (on a scale of 10). 

I had a lot of new stuff to be totally responsible for. 

A lot of fresh ways to muck everything up with a mountain of consequences to suffer when “shyte met fan.” 

So, I started looking into ways to murder my anxieties. 

And I discovered something that shocked me to my core:

To a large extent, we actually CHOOSE when to dump the anxiety hormones into our system.

So jittery doesn’t HAVE to be our constant default setting. 

Some people have physical chemical imbalances or particularly bad trauma that need extra attention from professionals. 

But there's a certain degree of choice most of the time. 

You can choose to boil over. 

With practice, you can also choose to remain calm. 

There are two ways to get control over this: 

(1) Bio-feedback:
With machines that measure your body's responses to anxiety-causing situations. You can watch the meters waggle into the red zone… and actually learn to counteract those responses while they're starting up. It works like gangbusters. (Countless therapists use bio-feedback.)

(2) Plain old Americanized Zen:
Sit quietly each day and relax your entire body. Melt into the chair (or floor, if you prefer to lay down), breathing slowly and deeply. 

And when you're totally relaxed…

… NAME this mellow state. And call it up to the plate whenever you start to feel the anxious jitters kicking in. 

And after a bit of practice, you can go to your relaxed zone whenever you want to. 

The key: 

Most anxiety-provoking situations require decisions to be made. 

But few of us can make good decisions when flustered.

Staying calm helps you look at your options with clarity so you can skip the freak-out. 

* You may come to the same conclusions on action. 
* Or you may think of something better. 

Being anxious doesn't make you smarter, or more clever, or more decisive. 

It just sends your system into overdrive, which wears it out.

Just realizing it IS a choice most of the time is step one. 

For more tips, check out the blog right here https://www.john-carlton.com/2020/05/how-to-murder-anxiety/.
