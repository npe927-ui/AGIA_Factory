Is it ever okay?Howdy.

The white noise of ad culture can get thick and evil and noisy. 

The disintegration of decency is sad, but there’s always a silver lining if you look for it….

… because the increased noise out there creates a need for quality, good content, and real personality.

And passion, of course.

All this is on my mind because, man, those kids over on Madison Avenue are getting even more obnoxiously clever.

Here’s me, surfing for news and my cursor accidentally hovered for a nanosecond.

That alone was enough to ignite their video, with very loud audio and enough rapid-fire editing to hurt my eyes.

Nothing new about any of that. 

But here’s the kicker: 

I couldn’t turn it OFF.

There’s no “X” delete button, no volume control, no pause switch. NOTHING.

And the video was looping — going through the same eruption of noise and movement every minute or so. 

It was easily the most irritating ad I’ve ever seen in my life. 

And that’s saying something.

I love it. 

It makes those annoying pop-ups seem quaint and innocent.

But does it WORK to bring in sales?

That question has always been the bugaboo with Madison Ave-type ads.

Now, this obnoxiousness MIGHT bring in a few short-term results. 

It’s a sad fact of life — aggressive behavior gets rewarded. 

Jerks who cut in line get faster service, and assholes who make a scene eventually get their way. 

There are seldom any consequences. 

This is the legacy of a culture that tolerates spoiled brats. (Old man rant over.)

Some misguided marketers see the success of obnoxious online ads as evidence that they, too, must get on that bandwagon. 

But smart ones would never, ever disrespect their audience this way. 

They get that the real key to building a list of rabid customers who will be your fans for life is shared passion.

And REAL communication and empathy.

That’s your ticket. 

(Passion is NOT the same as obnoxiousness, by the way. Lots of folks get confused between the two.)

When everyone else is shouting, speak softly and carry a big damn stick. 

The stick being knowledge of human psychology and the timeless fundamental secrets of marketing, of course.
