Essential lesson for all marketers who sell with wordsHowdy.

Here’s a bold opinion of mine that sometimes gets me in a whack of (good-natured) trouble:

The English language has produced the best patoi since our ancestors started grunting at each other.  

(French peoples, send me your hate.)

I think most folks would agree.

Unfortunately, the majority of our fellow citizens have vocabularies that ceased growing when they were around 12.  

That’s why newspapers and magazines write to an 8th grade level at most. 

(More often than not, they try to keep it to Grade 5 or lower.)

It’s also why prime time TV shows are on a non-stop mission to dumb things down even further.

This can be fine… 

… as long as communication still occurs. 

And as a marketer… as long as sales still occur.

I actually love using fancy words– but only when I’m sure I’m among folks who appreciate them.  

Most of my writing, and especially all of my teaching materials, are carefully scrubbed of fifty-cent words… 

… because being understood is priority one, especially when trying to sell.  

So never use a ball-buster from the Thesaurus when a nice piece of street slang will do the same job.

The trouble is…

… the culture is still pretty much stuck on the 9th floor of the Tower of Babble when it comes to being precise about important words.

I could write for days on this subject.

Confusion about words and what they mean can have you believing that you’re on the road to happiness…

… when you’re actually about to plunge head-first into a pit of misery.

So keep it simple and clear.

You may have heard this lesson from me before.

But you can NEVER underestimate the importance of communicating well if you want to succeed as an entrepreneur (or anything else.)
