Except for smart people, of course...Howdy.

Life as a biz owner or freelancer is a lot like being on a rollercoaster ride. 

The ultimate high and the thrill of victory. 

Plummeting into agony after screwing up yet again for the umpteenth time.

Wheeeee!

Would you have it any other way though? 

The first year or so is thrilling and heart-wrenching at the same time.

You get your first big check…

… and you’re so tickled that you can’t help but want to make a copy, frame it and stick it up on the wall for all to admire. 

Then growth happens.

This is a good thing.

But it’s also where almost everyone mucks it all up. 

I’m not just talking about how entrepreneurs get a good thing going and then complicate it to hell. 

(Although I talk about that a lot, cuz it happens ALL the time.)

This is a different problem that is also very COMMON. 

If you want your biz to grow, not stall…

… then it's time to stop doing it all yourself. 

Seriously. 

STOP.

Hire a freaking assistant, for crying out loud. 

Make use of your network to get perspective from colleagues and maybe find some staff. 

But most important of all…

Know when to get help from experts when the LACK of expertise is what’s holding you back. 

There's no shame in asking (or paying) for good advice. 

In fact, this is the ONE thing all top marketers have in common. 

They don't hesitate to invest in solving their problems (and learning new tricks) as they grow. 

I was a one-man band when I started out.

Fortunately, I also got to hang out with top pros who quickly taught me to scoff at the idea of going it alone. 

When they found someone who had the goods they didn't have yet, these legends didn't blink at the cost. 

They made a quick calculation: 

How much MORE can I make with the advice this expert shares with me? 

Too many entrepreneurs (even veteran biz owners) consider it a matter of pride or something. 

"I don't need no stinking help." 

It's how toddlers operate. 

Marketers throwing petty tantrums like this gets old fast.

It holds you back too. 

In other aspects of life, it may be best to keep your own counsel and go it alone.

But not in biz. That's a certain path to obliteration. 

All top marketers rely on experts and have no use for the foolishness of doing it all alone.

Cuz let’s face it:

Sometimes, all you need is a clue.
