Read this right now to get back on track...Howdy. 

No matter how good things were at one point, it’s easy to get all mixed up and turned around. 

Are you ready to right the ship again?

Good. 

I know I've sent these before, but a review of these 10 life-changing steps is always a good idea. 

Step 1: STOP wasting time.
Knock off just ONE TV show, and you’ve just found a hot little hour to devote to your new life. Tear the plasma monster off the wall, and you’ve recovered a second lifetime of hours.

Oh, and get off social media for a while. That place is quickly becoming a cess pit. 

And it can suck you in for hours as it destroys your faith in humanity. 

So log off for a while. 

Your happiness and productivity will thank you. 

Step 2:  Allow the dreaded “D” word into your world.
That stands for “discipline”… something few Westerners have even a nodding acquaintance with. Every day, do something you really, really, really don’t want to do (that needs to be done eventually). 

Step 3:  Be a good animal.
Eat better, treat sleep as a sacred necessity, get your butt into the gym or even on a trail. Also, allow for quality “ponder” time. And enjoy life with gusto. You could do worse than follow your dog’s lead in most of this. (Except for eating garbage, of course.)

Step 4: Face your fears.
If you’re not keeping a private journal where you can air out everything on your mind, then start one now.

Also: If you’ve got something bugging you that ain’t going away with simple pop psychology tricks, then get some pro help.  

There’s ZERO shame in it. 

Don’t let anyone tell you differently.

Step 5: Stop lying.
To others. And to yourself. 

This doesn’t mean you becoming that azzhole who constantly delivers “the truth” to everyone around them. But it’s perfectly all right to say “no comment”, or “um, yeah, I’m not gonna share that”. 

The “Magic Word” that all professionals and successful business owners have in their arsenal is “No”.  

Said politely, with a smile, but firmly and without explanation. 

Step 6: Do constant reality checks.
I often say that good salesmen lead better lives… because to make sales, you must see the world and everyone in it as it is. NOT as you wish it was, or believe it should be.

Step 7: Reach out to loved ones.
People miss you. Somebody’s waiting for a call from you, right now. Make it.

Step 8: Reach out to colleagues.
Every single successful marketer I know has a deep network of buddies and colleagues they call frequently, and share information that outsiders would pay a fortune for. It can even be shocking to realize how much support you can get from competitors. 

Business can be like a hockey game — brutal, but with total respect for the other team.

Step 9: Reach out to experts.
Mentoring changed my life. I’ve had multiple mentors along the way, and some didn’t even realize they WERE mentors. But all had fountains of knowledge and skills worth exploring and figuring out.

Step 10: Master the art of setting good goals.
Also, put together a doable plan to achieve them. Change doesn’t come easy — there are all kinds of obstacles, in real life and in your head. Fortunately, with just a little bit of coaching, you can become a goal-achieving monster.

There you have it.

Everything you need to know about how to get from where you are to where you want to be.

All that’s left is to get moving.

Stay frosty,
