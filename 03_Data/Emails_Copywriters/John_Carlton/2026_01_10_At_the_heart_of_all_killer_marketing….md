High level pro secrets await… Howdy.

No doubt you’ve heard the saying that all is fair in love and war.

Well, now you can add marketing and copywriting to that list.

Cuz it really is a bloody battlefield out there.

Practical advice is needed more than ever…

… but there’s a whole whack of wannabe gurus out there selling stuff to tickle your ears about what you want to hear…

… regardless of whether or not the tactics work in the real world. 

By now, you have undoubtedly encountered courses on "How to sell without selling" and all that.

Video marketing and AI have only added confusion to the mix when promoting your products and services.

As an old school dinosaur, I still prefer the written word.

Then again, I also know from personal experience that other media can kick ass.

But there’s a caveat. 

You’ve got to do it right.

All the tech whiz-bang is nothing if you lack a KILLER sales message that keeps audiences riveted to the page, or the video, or the webinar, or the podcast, or whatever. 

So let’s just clarify this:

Video is NOT magic.  

AI is NOT magic.

No one will give you money for your product just because your marketing is slick and polished and looks like you spent a million bucks on it.

There is more than one smokin’ hot vehicle for delivering a good sales message.  

Introducing video into your media mix can skyrocket results in most markets. 

But you gotta understand "why" before you can drill down on "how."

To a great extent, it just makes the message easier to digest.

Prospects who would otherwise be intimidated by long scrolling text can suddenly engage with visuals, audio, and all the attention-getting power of passion-inflected voice-overs.

So I repeat:

Technology rocks.  

And when something new hits that we can’t even fathom now…

… it will also rock.

When your monitor starts spraying you with carefully-selected odors...

... then smells will also become a marketing tool.  

(Taste may not be far off, either.)

The best kind of selling will always be a senses-consuming, totally customized experience for the prospect.

Just NEVER, ever fall into the trap of screwing up your message because you got your hands on a fancy new toy or gizmo.

A surge in sales will always be the result of classic salesmanship, no matter how you look at it. 

Above all, you must test everything vigorously. 

And if you discover that your video is killing it with conversions…

…  it’s NOT because your production is so smooth.

It’s always the message. 

Always.

A sucky message in video will still get you sucky results no matter how flashy or expensive the production.

And a humble salesletter can bring in big bucks by keeping it simple and conveying an irresistible pitch. 

It’s all about the psychology.
