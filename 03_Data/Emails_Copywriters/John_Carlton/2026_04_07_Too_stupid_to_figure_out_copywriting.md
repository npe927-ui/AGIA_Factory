In the meantime, I bet today's lesson will fill in some of those gaps and give your sense of worthiness a much-needed boost. 

This riveting tale begins with a woman named Arleen. (So buckle up. You already know this is going to be good.) 

Arlene was the first copywriter I’ve ever met. 

To say I was intrigued would be the understatement of the year. 

She was so… glamorous…

… spending her days coming up with captions and headlines for all the stuff my company sold.

How hard could it be? I asked myself. Soon, I knew without a shadow of a doubt that that was exactly the kind of work I should be doing. 

After much back and forth, I finally mustered up the courage to approach her.

“Hey, Arleen — how did you become a copywriter?”

Arleen turned and gave me a look of utter disgust, as though I just farted on her desk. 

“Forget it, John,” she said, her face wrinkled in contempt.

“It’s too hard. You will never figure it out.”

Ouch. That stung.

But it all worked out. 

Because her nasty insult fired me up and made me more determined than I had ever been about anything in my entire slacker life. 

It beat every other “self-help” or “productivity” tip I've ever encountered. (And believe me, I have seen and tried it all.) 

The rest is history, I suppose.

I never saw Arleen again.

Still, when I started raising my fees to obscene amounts, the level of satisfaction was beyond words.

The lesson?

If you’re feeling down and out, or someone has just (successfully) made you feel like a worthless failure…

… always remember that the underdog can and does win. 

All of the Big Players have had their share of humiliation, even if they prefer to keep it to themselves. 

Arleen wasn’t the only one who tried to knock me down as I rose up the ranks. 

But it was her throw-away insult that kicked my ass in all the ways I needed it kicked.

In large part, it made me who I am today. 

It would really frost her ass if she ever knew the outrageous amounts of money I’ve earned in spite of her efforts to tear me down. 

And yes, I take perverse pleasure in that thought.

Take that, Arleen, you would-be destroyer of dreams.

  
So today, if you can’t find a positive reason to move forward, just remember some BS that someone else has said to you, either recently or years ago. 

Blissful feelings are nice. 

But there’s also nothing wrong with negative motivation.

Whatever gets the job done, right?
