Great exercise to kick your brain into overdrive… Howdy.

Long copy still rules when it comes to selling product. 

That's why every effective copywriter has had the words, “the more you tell, the more you sell” drilled into their brains. 

But the world isn't perfect. 

Sometimes, you gotta squeeze your entire message into as few words as possible. 

But even if you have all the time in the world to tell your story, being forced to condense it all is a great exercise. 

Then, if you're working with those frustrating ad platforms that offer frustratingly small space, you can still outsell everyone arround you. 

Remember that readers have ever-shrinking attention spans. 

The ability to squeeze big ideas into tasty, bite-sized tidbits is an essential skill for ALL marketers. 

Everything you write has a hook that is the beating heart of the pitch. 

However long your final draft may be…

… it MUST sizzle with tight writing, action-packed sentences, and ZERO unnecessary words. 

Look for the defining hooks of each section.

Spot the word or phrase or imagery that nails the essence of the hook. 

Here’s what you’re aiming for with every word:

* Incite curiosity…

* Challenge reality…

* Demand that your reader click to find out more.

It all starts by asking yourself this simple question:

What are the emotional, financial, possibly spiritual, and certainly success-targeted fundamental hooks of your message? 

* “Free” is always a good word, when applicable. 

* Pattern interrupts are great. They challenge standard thinking, using word pictures that make folks take notice.

Know what the hooks are in your message and study the art of teasing readers into action.

I repeat: 

Boredom KILLS sales. 

So edit ferociously and MURDER those unnecessary words and tangents.
