Reducing objections to dust…Howdy.

You know the worst thing about having an unchecked belief system?

It’s the sense of false confidence they offer the believer. 

You can believe — with all your heart and soul — that you’re the baddest ass in the bar… the prettiest girl at the beach… or the savviest hustler on the street.

It’s your privilege to believe anything you want.

But things get ugly real fast when that belief runs up against reality. 

Smart marketers and sales pros work hard to keep their beliefs in check. 

But when dealing with a prospect, they never rail against what that person thinks is true (no matter how silly it may be). 

And they certainly don’t trust logic or reason to try to change someone’s mind.

Instead, they work with what they got.

Rookie salespeople shrivel away then they see a wall of objections and beliefs that run counter to buying their product.

On the flip side, a world-class sales pro couldn’t be happier than to hear ALL the reasons their prospect was refusing to buy.  

This is why old time door-to-door salespeople used to love seeing “No Solicitation” signs on the front door.

You know why? 

It meant that, once they got past the initial resistance, those homeowners were the easiest marks on any block.

And a rickety pile of objections was all that stood between them and their payday. 

Give a real pro an objection, and they reduce it to ashes.

All day long.

And when they’re done, the prospect thinks,  “Yeah, I do want that thing.”

This is why I want to teach salesmanship to everyone.

People who understand these concepts lead better lives. 

Not because they’re better people… 

… but because they are unencumbered with the burden of stupid beliefs.

They understand the process of selling that is going on in every store, on every website, in every magazine, on every TV station… 

… and between every set of humans alive — spouses, friends, neighbors, colleagues, enemies, and even strangers.

Trends come and go, but real salesmanship will never die.

Just be aware and challenge people’s beliefs at your own peril.

Stay frosty,
