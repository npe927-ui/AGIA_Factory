That means you too… Howdy.

I will never not be shocked by the amount of just plain nastiness running rampant in business.

One of the worst examples I’ve ever seen was a veep at an agency I was freelancing for. 

He had two staff writers under his cruel command.

He INSISTED they work in a small, windowless room with their desks facing each other. 

No talking. 

Ssshhhh. Get back to work. 

Which is why the idiotic tyrant was forced to hire expensive freelancers like me. 

But sometimes, that nasty person looking over your shoulder and nagging isn't an outside client. 

Often, it's your own critic inside your head making you feel guilty every minute you're not completely immersed in biz activity. 

As a freelancer, I refused to be stifled by anyone, including my own bad self. 

I gave myself permission to sleep until early afternoon and work all night because that's when I was the most productive.

Most importantly, I refused to force it. 

This freedom I insisted on is exactly why my ads worked when staff writers failed. 

So if you are subjecting yourself to a torturous, grinding schedule... 

... then take a lesson from advertising legend David Ogilvy, who believed in using the power of your unconscious to do the hardest tasks for you.

Here’s how it works:

* Load up on information about a project.

* Then go catch forty winks, telling your brain to have something for you when you wake up. 

The idea of "allowing" the unconscious mind free rein with anything important scares the hell out most folks.

It seems lazy, also spooky and weird, and yet… it's incredibly effective. 

I based my entire career on stuffing my head with info… 

… and then buggering off for a nap – however long it took. 

Twenty minutes or two hours, didn’t matter.

I just made sure I had a pen and paper nearby for when I woke up… 

… because the headlines and copy came gushing out afterward. 

There is a real "hidden genius" inside all of us — our unconscious mind.

Top producers have never scoffed at this idea.

Instead, they laugh all the way to the bank.

So go take a break and let your unconscious work for you this weekend.
