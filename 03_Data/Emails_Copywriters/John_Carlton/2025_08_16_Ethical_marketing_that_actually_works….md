Throughout my career, I’ve witnessed mobs of “nice-guy-and gal” entrepreneurs knocked aside by conniving, quasi-sociopathic competitors…

… shucksters who brag shamelessly (without the actual chops to back it up).

It's so freaking unfair!

Just like everything else in life.

Nevertheless…

You CAN still rise to the top as one of the "good guys".

Authenticity can get you further in your career than just BSing people will. 

However…

… you will NEED some deeper-level understanding of how to be authentic in the sea of phony baloney BS we're all drowning in.

Best rules to follow: 

* Know who you are

* Understand how what you offer fits into the life of your prospect 

* Sell yourself (and your biz) with pizzazz and persuasive mojo.

Whatever new and shiny tech unfolds, basic old school salesmanship will always be your BEST FRIEND…

… especially if you want to maintain your authenticity and integrity while hitting the big time with your biz. 

People fail every day because they HATE the idea of selling.

But get this: 

You already sell yourself, every minute of every day.

Remember:

* You sold yourself to your Significant Other (who would never have even dated you if you hadn't made a case for your sorry ass).

* You've sold yourself, and whoever else you dragged in, on every project and goal you've ever gone after.

* And if you're anywhere near the biz world…you're selling all day long in ways you may not even realize.

And your authenticity?

Yep. You're selling that, too.

The difference is: Yours is real.

Which now becomes a HUGE advantage against the lying, fake, evil bastards you’re up against.

So no, being “one of the good ones” won’t get you anywhere all on its own. 

You still have to learn to sell. 

It's the key to a good ride.

And when you couple your newfound skills with actually being a good person…
