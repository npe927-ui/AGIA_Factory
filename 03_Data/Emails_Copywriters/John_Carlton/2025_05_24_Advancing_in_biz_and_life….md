More high-level secrets for you…Howdy. 

One of the primary skills in  business is networking…

… which takes the ability to get along with people. 

Simple, but oh so complicated. Especially when there’s a personality clash or a stark difference of opinions.

That’s why, when I talk about developing advanced sales skills,  I stress the "bonding" process so much. 

Your market is people, whatever product or service you’re selling. 

Your associates are also people. 

And while it is technically possible to be an asshole and win… 

… you can’t be a clueless asshole and win.

And it’s not advisable at any rate. 

I’m at the age where I’ve dealt with a lot of people coming up the ranks who have never been told "no" in their lives. 

It’s a trait you don’t see as much in older people, because they’ve had more of an opportunity to get bitch-slapped by the universe.

Old people can be amazingly arrogant, of course… 

… but it’s often a savvier kind of arrogance. They know they have the accumulated life lessons to back it up.

It’s shocking how other people just crumble under the mildest of assaults.

Life, to this point, has taught them that an arrogant attitude works. 

Because it does, but only to an extent. 

Consider that most folks recoil from any kind of confrontation…

… which means there is little initial feedback for the person ramming their way through life with blunt arrogance.

Being an asshole doesn’t make biz success easier, it just makes it seem that way, at least in the short term. 

I was arrogant, once. 

But I didn’t become a successful freelancer until I’d been fired from numerous jobs, and my ego was shattered by being told "no" a thousand times. 

I never liked it… 

… but it steeled me against the slings and arrows of outrageous fortune once I did go after success.

Without that experience of being told "no", I wouldn’t be where I am today.

Accepting defeat (temporarily) and polishing up your skills then trying again is probably the single most important lesson you can learn when you’re young and arrogance otherwise comes so easily. 

A little humility doesn’t stunt your abilities to get stuff done.

But it does help you shrug off the small shyte.

Just a word to the wise.
