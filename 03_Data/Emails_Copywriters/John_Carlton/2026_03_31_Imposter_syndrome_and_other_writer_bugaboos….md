Stop sabotaging yourself right now… Howdy.

After years in the trenches, I’ve discovered a secret about successful marketers and entrepreneurs. 

We may seem on top of the world, brimming with self confidence and competence.

But the outside doesn’t always match the inside.

From personal experience, I can tell you that even though I always “knew”  I could get the job done… 

… I still suffered crippling bouts of doubt and anxiety during the creative process.

This used to freak me out. 

The first few times I went through it — before I understood what was happening — I took it personally. 

At first I thought I was just inadequate, and a piss-poor excuse for a professional.

Imposter Syndrome at its finest.

Then I discovered that ALL the best writers and marketers (as well as most other “creative” types) go through this identical stage during the process of crafting something new.

Learning this calmed me down considerably. 

And I learned to keep it in perspective.

Nobody has EVER died from the angst of creating something. 

So I’m telling you, as others told me: 

Don’t freak out. 

ALL writers go through a period of brain-twisting insanity while crafting good stuff, especially under deadline pressures. 

(I haven’t tested this, but if you’re not going through at least some discomfort, you may not be pushing yourself hard enough. Naughty writer, cheating your client.)

But knowing the “no pain, no gain” rule, I have learned to just buckle down and do what needs doing to get the job done.

I even learned to enjoy the exhaustion of pushing myself to new levels.

Sadistic monster that I am.
