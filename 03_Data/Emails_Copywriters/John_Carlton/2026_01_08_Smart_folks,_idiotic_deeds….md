This nonsense must stop...Howdy.

It’s insane just how many urban myths can remain part of a human’s core belief system…

…even LONG after they’ve been thoroughly debunked with annoying facts.

It’s not just “dumb” folks either. 

We ALL do it.

Yes, even you, you brainiac.

So no one is allowed to feel smug.

Marketers and copywriters, however, need to be acutely aware of these bugaboos swirling around in the collective unconsciousness.

Fun story: 

Back in my college days, I had a girlfriend who was certifiably smart as hell. I was in awe of her brilliance, in fact. 

Yet somehow, she convinced a bunch of us to get involved with a chain letter pyramid scheme.

She talked herself — and us — into it with seemingly impeccable logic.

Smart folks across the country fall for what should be the most obvious scammiest of scams.  

One “smart” business woman I knew bragged about refinancing her third home so she could buy another one…

… moments before the real estate market turned into a yet another bloodbath. 

She was certain she was about to strike it rich…

… because ALL she had to do was find folks willing to pay her mortgages through high rents.

Simple. Right?

But don’t judge.

ALL of us have a belief system that governs our behavior.

Rarely are any of these systems rooted in the way the universe actually operates. 

Which brings us back to marketing and biz.

Almost every single consultation I’ve ever been paid for included an extended session where I had to beat the assumptions out of the client.

“No,” he’d say, “I didn’t try that tactic, because I just assumed that…” is what I hear the most.  

The words “I just assumed” were always followed by complete nonsense.

As a young punk too smart for my own good, I used to pull out encyclopedias to triumphantly present the actual correct answer to whatever was being angrily discussed.

What I learned: 

Presenting facts — even unimpeachable stacks of figures, statistics, quotes and conclusions — NEVER dampens the enthusiasm another person has for what he “believes” to be true.

This is why biz owners and copywriters CANNOT afford to leave anything out of a sales pitch.

Top-tier marketers use long copy formats, whether through written ads, websites, autoresponder email series, videos, or speeches…

… because they know they must supply both rational and irrational reasons to buy. 

The next time you try to persuade someone to do something (including buying your product, providing their email, or coming to your event)...

…  always throw in a few nods to the nonsense you suspect is roiling inside their brain.

And remember that what you know for a FACT is true may be utterly contradicted by what your prospect believes is true.

Bet you didn’t know there was so much psychology involved in selling a product, right?
