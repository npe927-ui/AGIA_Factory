Listen to the old dude, would ya…Howdy.

As a non-stop student of all things marketing and salesmanship related, you encounter a lot of theory. 

A lot of it is untested BS. 

I like to hold fast to an old Zen maxim that states “there is no "trying" in reality.”

It’s a harsh fact that veteran marketers have always understood deep down.

You're either eating a sandwich, or not eating it.

To even think you're "trying" to eat it is absurd to anyone who understands how things get done.

If you crave success…

… you gotta take over the writing and management of your life's script.

It’s a lot like being in a movie. 

You are the star of your own life.

(Also the producer, scriptwriter, director, and film crew.)

That’s not to say that you’ll ever be totally in control. 

You won’t. 

But you have a lot MORE control than you think.

Write your script the way you want, and then go for it.

Accept reality, but never accept your own lame excuses for not making things happen with as much input from you as you could muster. 

Phrases like "I can't do that" always have a sinister translation to “I WON’T do that.” 

Or its equally toxic cousin, "I don't have time for that…" 

… which translates to “I won't MAKE time for that.”

Those sayings make some people feel better about a lack of progress.

But they don’t cut it in reality. 

There ain’t no malevolent third party interfering with your life. (Even if it does feel that way sometimes.)

Think of failure as yet another stepping stone to success. 

Cuz that’s all it really is…

… a long journey of small, tiny steps taken consistently. 

It’s rarely a giant leap. 

And we’re constantly course correcting.

There’s a lesson in every mistake and failure somewhere for you that will help you next time.

Top achievers also realize that failure is NEVER the final act. 

You get as many sequels, do-overs, and replays as you desire.

If you're ready to control your destiny a bit…

... then get busy figuring out what you need to fix or change.

Maybe you do it badly, at first. 

That’s still “doing”, you know. 

And then you learn to “do” better. 

So don't "try". 

Do.

Just like Yoda said.

You gotta admit the old dude had a point. 

Okay, carry on.

Stay frosty,
