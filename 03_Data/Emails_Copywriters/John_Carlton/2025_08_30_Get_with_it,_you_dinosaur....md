Howdy.

Do you ever get in your head about coming up with marketing materials for your products and launches?  

Here's an undying principle to always keep you profitably on the straight and narrow: 

Long form sales copy still works like crazy.

But now that I've drilled that into your brain, let's get
you unstuck:

STOP calling what you're writing a sales letter.

Banishing the term is a quick tip for getting out of your own way. 

Most marketers actually don't even use the term anymore.  

Instead, we talk about creating a sales message.  

It could be: 

* A physical letter

* A landing page

* A video

* A series of auto-responders

Or it might be a speech from a stage, or a webinar, teleseminar or whatever.

When you can sell, you never have to sweat the "writing" part ever again.  

You can do everything verbally and have it transcribed into video scripts, or websites, or printed ads, or whatever you need.

Or hire someone else to do it or even program your trusty chatbot.

I CANNOT emphasize the following enough:

It's not the "writing" part that matters.  

It's the "SALES" part that matters.

Once you get that, you breeze past any hurdles or mental roadblocks you have about "writing." 

But what if you aren't a natural sales pro?  

Then learn. 

It's
not voodoo.  

It's actually easy, when you have an experienced guide.

However... if you never bring yourself up to speed...

... then you will forever be prey to marketers who DO know how to sell.

And they will sell you one Magic Box solution after another.  

Solutions they themselves have succeeded with...

... because - oops, they forgot to tell you - they are top-notch sales pros.

The best marketers out there are obsessed with closing the deal.

You're just not in the game if you don't understand salesmanship.

You are, in fact, meat.  Sustenance for those who do know understand how to persuade and influence and sell.

So before you try ANY of the clever new tactics out there...

... for your own sake... 

... learn how to sell.
