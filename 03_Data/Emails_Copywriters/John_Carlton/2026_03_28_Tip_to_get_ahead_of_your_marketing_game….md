Anyway, back to that unfortunate dude from the Middle Ages.

If he was a natural salesperson, he’d STILL be able to survive and thrive today. (Once he stopped screaming about all the sorcery and witchcraft otherwise known as technology, that is.) 

Marketers who understand this and are clued into timeless human nature are way ahead of the game. 

They don’t have to struggle to wonder "what the kids want" in new products…

,,, and they don’t regard people older than them as grouchy alien beings with unknowable needs.

Don't get me wrong; I love modern life...

... but I liked it just as much before personal computers and the Internet came along. 

I’m rolling with the punches… armed with the knowledge (earned from reading biographies) that things will ALWAYS change.

And there will always be a way to adapt and thrive.

One of the things I remember about being young is that — as a teenager — more stuff will happen to you in a day, than will happen to an adult in a month.

When you’re still full of piss and vinegar, that’s fun. 

I liked living through radically new adventures each week, never knowing where I’d wake up Sunday morning.

But I also liked settling into middle age, and getting into productive routines as a veteran writer and marketer. 

I can finally take longer views of things, and plan ahead. 

What a concept.

With perspective, it all comes into focus. 

Your market is one long passing parade.

It can look like a disorganized mob scene if you don’t understand the fundamentals of how people live their lives.

Here it is in a nutshell:

People are people. 

Their needs, dreams and fears haven’t changed much since the dawn of time.

What you learn — about yourself, and about your fellow human — will help you become a better communicator and (if you pay attention) a killer sales pro.
