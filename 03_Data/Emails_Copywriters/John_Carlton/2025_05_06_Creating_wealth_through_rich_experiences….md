But are you limiting your potential and ability to connect with others based on silly, inane notions of the types of people you ought to be hanging out with?

I said "hello" to them all.

Most ignored me.

Kids who grow up oblivious of anyone outside their peer group will tend to become isolated: intellectually, emotionally, physically. 

They end up with problems adjusting to change and challenges.

Mind you, a few of the kids did smile and say “hello” back.

And those are the ones I'd bet on to succeed faster and with more aplomb.

All biz revolves around human connections, no matter how digital the world becomes.

Eventually, I realized I was cheating myself by limiting my exposure to people outside my usual mob.

My best friends, at one point, were Gary Halbert, 12 years older than me, and Stan Dahl, 12 years younger. 

We got along just fine, and the age differences were never a big thing.

In fact, I became biz partners with both of them, and remain successfully partnered up with Stan to this day.

I have multiple Millennial friends, and still go out of my way to hang with older folks.

It’s a rich world out there, beyond your habits and same-old, same-old.
