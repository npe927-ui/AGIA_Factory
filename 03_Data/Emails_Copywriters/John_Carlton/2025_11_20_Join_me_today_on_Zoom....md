I learned this cool psychological trick early on from an old-school way of thinking:…

(and maybe this isn’t going to land well in this day and age)

… but it worked for me, so here it is:

Imagine there’s a “Gun To Your Head” when creating ads.

If there was a gun to my head that would go off if my ad didn’t work…

… would I still use that headline?
… that sales message? 
… that word in that paragraph? 

… or any other risky tactic to FUBAR the chances of the little ad I was sending out into the cold, cruel world?

Being clever, or funny, or taking grandiose risks with a client’s advertising was out of the question.

Instead, I stuck with the tried and true that has worked since the beginning of history, and has never stopped working (not even for a moment).

Some of my colleagues considered brass salesmanship beneath them and unworthy of their talents.  

Their job was to be clever and creative.

Meanwhile:

My ads worked, theirs bombed, and suddenly I was in high demand.

I kept this quote by the painter Renoir handy, because he knew what he was talking about: 

“First, learn your craft.  It won’t prevent you from becoming a genius later.“  

Huge wisdom there, you know.

Today’s problems might be different.

But there is always a way to get something done. 

Always.

I’ve always refused to be unethical, or do anything illegal… 

… so a lot of the imaginary scenarios that burble up aren’t something I would ever do in the real world.  

But coming up with crazy ideas strengthens your brain’s ability to problem solve, whether or not you actually implement the plan as is. 

Just thinking about them is part of the creative process.

Dream up every single way you MIGHT be able to get past the natural barriers to solving the problem.

Whether you act on the inspiration doesn’t matter…

… the point is:

You have proven to your brain that it CAN be done.

No matter what marketing or biz problem you’re facing…

… there’s always a way to solve it.

The trick is to just find a way to do it that doesn’t involve bloodshed, jail time, or losing sleep at night.
