 What smart pros are always on the lookout for… Howdy.

What is it with some people that they are simply incapable of saying "I don’t know"?

It’s not like there’s any shame in not knowing an answer.

But there is TERRIFIC shame in making something up, as if your imagination and your desire to be a know-it-all could mold reality to your whims.

People lie.

I get that.

There’s no point in getting upset about it because it’s a behavior preprogrammed into the human hard drive.

But you can work on your own "lie radar". 

Simply make a mental note of what someone tells you, and then check it out. 

This doesn’t mean putting on a cape and becoming an avenging angel punishing everyone for every little lie they tell, usually to save face. 

You don’t "win" anything by confronting a liar, for the most part. 

And it’s not your job to set everything straight in the world. 

When you independently verify a fact, the person telling the fib usually doesn’t even need to KNOW that you’ve found them out. 

But you know.

In my experience, liars don’t often "get away" with much, over the long haul. 

Sure, they’ll reap the short-term benefits, but they’re living a spiritually unhealthy life… 

… and it catches up. Eventually.

The Zen warrior would rather learn the truth in secret than share in a communal lie. 

It can be a lonely road to travel, at first.

But when you surround yourself with honorable people, the truth is always welcome.

Then, you can even forgive small transgressions (since you don’t act on their version of things without fact-checking everything first, anyway).

Sure, it’s complex. 

Tangled webs and all that.

Just start working right away on your intuitive radar.

It’s a skill ALL top marketers possess after years of disciplined practice and follow-through.
