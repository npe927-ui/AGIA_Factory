No funny business, now…Howdy.

Should copywriters be funny?

It’s an honest question, because a lot of them are. 

Many of my fellow copywriters can make your gut hurt with laughter.

As master communicators https://www.john-carlton.com/2011/07/how-to-communicate-with-humans/, it kinda goes with the territory.

Nevertheless, they’ll clam up and get dead serious in a hurry when it comes to crafting marketing messages. 

Avoiding humor like the plague is one of the first fundamental truths they learn about in copywriting and ad creation.

Why?

To quote the great David Ogilvy:

“People do not buy from clowns.”

Indeed, top ad writers almost never insert humor into their sales copy. 

Almost.

Like every rule, there are exceptions.

But if your goal is to make money with your copy, it’s good to stay on the serious side the vast majority of the time.

And make no mistake. 

Money is serious biz. 

Especially when the economy has gone bonkers and people start holding on to their wallets with white-knuckle urgency. 

Buyers who are NOT looky-loos buy because they have a problem.

And they NEED a solution, like yesterday.

As such, they aren’t usually keen on being the butt of a joke. 

Now, I like to think I’m a fairly humorous fella.

I’ve made someone snort coffee through their nose as recently as –  well, yesterday. 

But I might as well be a mortician when I’m ready to sit down and create a sales funnel. 

Losing a sale because you screwed around is NOT funny. 

And what makes something funny anyway?

Ivy League universities actually studied humor, trying to get a baseline of what makes people laugh, and why.

And their first biggest discovery?

Many folks literally do NOT have a sense of humor at all. None.

Sure, they may hold their bellies and laugh hysterically out loud.

But they’re faking it.

It’s a social cue, nothing more. 

The rule in bars is “no talking about politics or religion”  because it leads to fisticuffs.

For marketers, you can add “no funny stuff” to that list. 

You simply cannot predict what any list will find funny, not funny, offensive or just plain baffling.

Make sense?

Now go sell something…

… and save the jokes for your down time.
