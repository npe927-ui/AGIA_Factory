Recently I reencountered a classic biz tactic that I haven’t come across in ages.

Marketers, listen up…

… because this lesson carries a ton of wisdom.

It came from a conversation about lowering refund rates in biz.

Most people try to appease pissed off customers just by offering a refund.

Sure, it makes the problem go away quickly and without fuss. 

But what if you’re actually missing a chance to save the relationship and keep that happy customer coming back again?

So before just giving them their money back…

… have you tried offering them a better deal, an extra bonus (to the extent of ethical bribery) to keep them in your tribe? 

These are all fairly common tactics that can be effective. 

But they also highlight a deeper principle. 

"Any problem that can be solved with money…

… can be solved without money."

Value doesn’t always equal greenbacks. 

Using cash to solve something shows that you’ve stumbled upon a value level where the unhappy customer can be coaxed back into the fold.

Once you’ve established that the waffling customer WILL come back, for the right value…

… then you’re free to experiment with things other than cash on the barrelhead.

Like free stuff that’s valuable to the customer, even if it’s just laying around the office for you.

Too many biz owners get caught up in a black-and-white world of cash versus no cash.

However, a savvy entrepreneur seeks to create value with other things (from free reports, to an hour of your time on the phone, to free membership in a group, to, well, lots and lots of things).

You can avoid touching the honeypot while still providing value that’s just as good as cash.

Make sense?

A few bucks can get you past some hard-to-solve problems.

But when throwing moolah around cuts into your bottom line, thinking in terms of value instead can reframe the entire situation…

... and keep your customer AND your bottom line happy.
