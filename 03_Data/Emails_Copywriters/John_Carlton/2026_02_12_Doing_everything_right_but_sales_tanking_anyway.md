Howdy.

Crafting a masterful sales message is a lot like tip toeing through a field littered with landmines. 

There are so many psychological hurdles to overcome.

If you don't address the hidden fears and objections, sales start circling the toilet.

The worst part is that prospects don't tell you the real reason they don't
buy...

... often because they're not even aware of it themselves. 

Here's a big one: 

People are so terrified of being on the losing end of a humiliating experience... 

... that they will spin facts and truth into oblivion to back up their preferred reality.  

Everybody spins.  

I spin.  You spin. We all spin...

We spin to our good friends, to our enemies, to strangers, lovers, and our pets. 

We even do it with inanimate objects. 

"C'mon, car," I said to my car once on a cold morning.  

"Start, just start today, that's all I ask... and I'll wash and wax you and spit-shine your chrome and..."  

... total spin there.  

I've never waxed a car in my life.  I just want the damn thing to start.

But the biggest spin of all... 

... is the spin we deliver to ourselves.

How does all this apply to marketing?

Everybody wants the best possible deal.  Everybody.

You have to know how to enable the other guy to spin things inside his head so he believes he got
a more than fair deal.

Smart salespeople know that you can obliterate every obvious objection in a prospect's mind...

... but you'll still lose the sale if you don't appeal to the emotional side of the brain.

This is why the great copywriters have always shoveled massive payloads of bullets (explaining feature-benefit stories) into pitches.
You just never can predict which bullet will trigger that "Okay, what the hell, let's do this deal" response.

I leave you with this story that illustrates the point perfectly:

I once witnessed a musician selling his guitar to someone he knew.

These guys KNEW each other... 

... and the axe was worth ten times what the guy wanted for it.  

But still the buyer negotiated hard, working him down until it wasn't even a bargain anymore.

It was financial slaughter.  But the seller needed the money, so the deal went down.

Wanna know the crazy thing?

Both of these guys worked it out in their minds that it was fair and satisfying deal
for them.

The takeaway?

Never expect "logic" to win the day.

When you're finalizing your sales process, rational thought is the first casualty.

Pretty messed up, but that's the inner workings of the human mind for you.

Just another tidbit for your arsenal. 

Stay frosty,
