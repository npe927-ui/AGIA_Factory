Go for it, you amazing person, you... Howdy.

Caring too much about what other people think zaps both your ability to succeed.

It can also suck any and all joy out of the well-deserved reward.

One of the best things you can do for yourself is to stop caring what petty people think about you.

Sure, this seems like basic advice. Another tidbit that is sooo much easier to state than to put into practice.

So forget the fear of “looking” stupid. 

Get out there and BE as stupid as you want. 

Dance if you wanna dance. 

Sing. 

Write that next chapter of your book, spelling and grammar errors and all. 

Be silly and ecstatic and spontaneously embarrassing when it pleases you.

Hide out if that feels right. 

Above all. love with abandon. 

This is your mission, for the rest of this month and for next year. 

Succeed at this one thing, and you won’t just live a happier life.

You’ll actually be much smarter and more effective…

… because you won’t make dumb decisions just to fit in.

All of this will also prime you for a better biz and greater wealth.

So learn how to stare down the nay-sayers. 

And most of all…

... give yourself enough slack for mistakes that soon become lessons learned.

Hey, you screwed up. 

Ain’t none of us perfect. 

We all botch life, and we do it often.

The difference is:

Do you handle it like a toddler?

Or get busy fixing things like a mature pro?

It’s simple.

Just do the following as much as humanly possible:

– Clean up your mess
– Make amends 
– Fix what's broken. 

If you need new tools or behaviors, go find them.

Do not PROMISE to do better next time. 

DO better next time. 

Move forward. Grow.

And do it with raw joy and reckless abandon. 

Laugh at the squares, trapped in their self-made prisons of repression.

Ignore what others think or kill them with kindness, and keep moving.

Perfection has never been required, and it never will be.

Go be you, you amazing person, you.

Stay frosty,

John
