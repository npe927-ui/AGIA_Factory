Howdy. 

There have been oodles of rules telling us all how to live since the dawn of time. 

Marketers particularly love Sun Tsu’s “Art of War”. 

There’s good biz advice to be had...

... but it's a bit lopsided on the “kill first” side of things, at least for my tastes.

If all you read are hard-core “greed is good” manuals, you might succeed at making lots of money… 

… but there’s a good chance you’ll be alone and miserable at the end of the day. 

Which kind of defeats the point of getting rich in the first place.

The key to a good life is to strike that balance between a take-no-prisoners attitude combined with hope and empowerment. 

First, you learn the rules. 

Then you understand why they exist. 

Then you’ll know when, why, and how to break them to achieve glorious success. 

With that in mind, here are three of the big “inviolate” rules that you are probably in violation of. 

Rule #1. 
No good deed goes unpunished.

Americans, in general, are generous to a fault — despite our bad PR in the world. 

But you should NEVER do something with the expectation of being rewarded. 

* First, because that diminishes the act of kindness.

* And second… because doing something nice for someone often kickstarts a thought process where that person believes you OWE them even more. 

The psychological roots of this weird thinking are deep, and I could talk about it for days. But never mind. 

Just remember that being generous doesn’t require that you be a sucker.

Rule #2: 
All clients suck.

All clients suck because the very nature of being a client means you want something from the person you’ve hired. 

And they want something from you. 

It’s an adversarial relationship. 

Each side feels they gave it up too cheap.

When you’re your own client, then YOU suck. The rule holds true.
Writing for yourself is one of the hardest jobs you’ll ever take on… where you gotta kick your own ass, and set your own deadlines.

(Quick plug: Learning to create killer copy at lightning speed will help clients not suck quite so much, and here’s a great resource https://m190.isrefer.com/go/hsc/jcemail/ to fast track your way to success.)

Rule #3: 
Do NOT go into business with friends.

I broke that one a long time ago when I got into biz with my good friend Stan. 

Fortunately, it worked out.

But I’ve also seen 30-year friendships burst asunder, long-term marriages collapse, and even brothers never speak to each other again… 

… all after making the decision to go into business together.

If you decide to violate this well-established rule, there are ways to protect your relationship.

It starts by building a wall between it and your business life.

Personally, if a biz partnership threatens my friendship, I will kill the association where it stands.

Friends as biz partners can be done. 

It’s not easy, though. Most people screw it up and lose the relationship.

So yeah, we do need the stinkin’ rules.

We also need the wisdom to decipher when & whether to follow them.

And that's always the tricky part.
