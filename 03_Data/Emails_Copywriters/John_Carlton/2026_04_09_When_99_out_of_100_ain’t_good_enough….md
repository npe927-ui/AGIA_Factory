A big seething pile of negativity to avoid at all costsHowdy.

Success in anything is as much about mental prowess as it is about skill. 

Here's a good rule to keep in mind: 

NEVER allow your list of failures to build up.

That’s why you start small when setting goals. 

A simple objective, easily achieved, adds fuel to your mojo…

…  and gets you fired up for bigger and better successes. 

Failure has the opposite effect. 

You’ve probably noticed this. 

Because you can accomplish 99 of the 100 items on your To-Do-List.

… but if one gets pushed to tomorrow, it becomes a seething pile of negative energy.

No matter how hard you work or how much you achieve, you’ll always feel like you’re not as productive as you should be. 

Even worse, you’re nurturing a habit of not getting the most critical stuff done.

There are three ways to handle this catastrophe:

* Do it now. Add it to your growing list of successes. 

* If you can’t do it now, then give yourself a deadline or time TO do it. 

* When in slump, give yourself small projects to achieve and allow your successes to pile up again. 

Note: 

* Avoid cramming your day until you collapse with exhaustion at all costs.

* Keep your goals simple, straightforward and clean so you achieve them.

But chances are, you could use a little better planning.

So just grow up and take care of it.

Then you can get back to the fun stuff, free of that nagging sense you're a procrastinating slacker.
