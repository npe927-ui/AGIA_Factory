Why people just don't "get" your brilliance...Howdy.

Do you know what’s infuriating?

When you’re all hyped up about a mind-blowing experience, and you’re excitedly telling your peers all about it… 

… only to be greeted by blank stares. 

It’s even worse when you’re a marketer trying to inspire enthusiasm for a product you really believe in. 

No matter how intense any experience is for you or how passionately you tell the tale…

... other folks just don’t "get it." 

They just can’t see what all the fuss is about or why they should pay you any attention at all. 

Here’s a personal example:

As a wide-eyed youth, I used to "smile and nod" at the grizzled guys trying to impart their rough-and-tumble tales to me.

"Dude, like, Woodstock was freakin' incredible!"

Yeah, sounds like you had a bitchin' time.

Yawn.

You feel the sting of indifference most when telling your own story.

It’s not just that everyone’s attention spans are growing shorter as we’re all losing brain cells while doped out on social media…

… but now, there’s an almost impenetrable wall of cynicism and downright hostility to get through. 

Yelling louder ain’t cutting it anymore.

Nor will thumping on the table or jabbing the listener in the chest to punctuate your points. 

Luckily, there’s a trick to getting your message across. 

Effective communication (and successful sales pitches) use a deep psychological principle…

… one that eludes newbies and even intermediate marketers when trying to craft messages that capture attention.

First, you gotta understand this critical point:

Your passion has zero impact.

People are selfish. 

So they won’t care if they sense for one hot second that your spiel is all about you, you, you. 

Your experiences are the source of the details…

… but here's the secret to truly great writing that pulls people in: 

You still have to find a way to make it all about the listener.

Weave the "who, when, where, and what" into their life…

… in a way that brings up emotions, chemical dumps, goosebumps, and personal fears, thrills and chills.

Because your audience will only pay attention when they see themselves as the star of the story.

Stay frosty,

John
