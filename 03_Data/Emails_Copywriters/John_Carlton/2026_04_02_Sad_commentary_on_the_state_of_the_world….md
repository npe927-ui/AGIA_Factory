Great marketing lesson though… Howdy.

All marketers should take the time to read through brain studies.

Absolutely fascinating stuff. 

For example:

German and Dutch scientists have run experiments tracking eye movement and brain activity while the subject listens to radio ads.

Turns out, advertising is very effective at lighting up the cerebral cortex, amygdala and brainstem. 

On the other hand, imagine if they used classic literature like Moby Dick for their tests. Then, they’d have to adjust for the unfortunate fact that many people doze off in the presence of a book.

A sad commentary on the state of the world, maybe. 

But I’m not here to judge. I’m here to create marketing that works and to show you how to do the same. 

The fact is that EVERYONE is affected by good ad copy in some way. 

Negative or positive, people react. 

This is why it’s so important to learn the “background” secrets behind truly effective ads. 

As a marketer, you’re not just after a reaction…

… you’re after a very specific reaction. 

You’re gunning for a lead, a sale, or anything you can use in whatever capitalistic enterprise you’re engaged in.

And here’s one of the biggest of those background secrets: 

Which, if you’ve been on my list for any length of time, should not be a surprise at all: 

It’s all about salesmanship.

Not cleverness.

Not humor.

No pyrotechnics are necessary, or ANY of the silliness that most advertising indulges in.

Aspiring copywriters love the idea of hiding behind a computer screen and cranking out words to an audience they’ll never have to speak to in person. 

One of my toughest lessons to get across is this: 

If you want to be successful at the gig...

... you MUST learn the art of selling something to another human being. 

I’m not saying you have to quit your job and work in sales for the next few years. Although that would really help…

… because almost anyone can be persuasive enough to get someone to say “Yeah, that sounds really nice.” 

Master copywriters up the intensity by 1000 degrees. 

They can get the prospect to go from “pretty cool” to “Wow. How can I get one of those RIGHT NOW?”

Remember: 

The Marketing Graveyard is crammed to bursting with fabulous products and inventions and ideas that died because no one figured out how to sell them.

Whether it’s a product, a service, or an idea that can save humanity… 

… you still have to sell it before it will take off.

When you have this skill, the world is your playground.
