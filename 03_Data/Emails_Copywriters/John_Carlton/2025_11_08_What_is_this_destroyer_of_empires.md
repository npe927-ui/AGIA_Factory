Hint: it can also build them up, bigger and better…Howdy.

We’ve all heard that old cliche that  "money can't buy happiness."

Indeed, there are lots who are dripping with wealth that are miserly and miserable.

But being broke sucks too.

I can tell you that from personal experience.

It makes that moment when you finally score some Big Bucks even sweeter.

But… 

It can all go downhill fast.

Our brains are not geared for sustained happiness. 

We're built to handle 4 seasons:

* To metaphorically and physically freeze our butts off

* Then marvel at spring's renewal (and get romantic) 

* Laze about during the Dog Days of Summer

* And then gear up for the big changes of autumn. 

Good times, bad times.

Repeat.

This is why so many entrepreneurs ride the roller coaster of boom and bust. 

We work so hard for success and happiness only to sabotage the heck out of everything. 

That’s cuz we have complicated feelings about the good times.

Don't scoff.

The mental game has forever been the biggest destroyer of empires.

So, consider yourself warned.

Achieving great wins bring momentary bliss…

… but it doesn't last.

Smart entrepreneurs know this, and plan accordingly. 

Your list of goals should NEVER end with "Done. Happy ever after."

Life doesn't work that way.

Rather, constant forward movement that brings rewards and satisfaction is the way to go. 

Aim to understand your needs to be healthy... to feel spiritually connected to the world... to have your emotional world stable…

Above all, know "how much is enough" with the Big Bucks.

We aren't born with that kind of knowledge.

You gotta figure it out as you go.

Hint: Ignore what makes your buddies happy. They're probably lying to themselves, or at least mesmerized with the wrong goals.

This is one instance where tending your own garden pays off huge.

Find out what makes YOU happy. And go for it.

Also, find out what keeps you fulfilled (a different emotion, by the way). And go after that, too.

And, finally…

… figure out what you need to pay attention to that doesn't make you immediately happy…but is nevertheless critical to living a good life.

That's health, mostly. And embracing love. And knowing the joys of giving to others, and not letting your ego control your actions.

Yeah, it's a full plate of stuff to obsess on.

But it's how you get closer to true happiness.

Stay frosty,

John
