What to do when sales tank… Howdy.

One of the toughest challenges in writing copy is soothing people’s fear of being "taken."

This instinct is basically an almost-impenetrable psychological "electric fence" around the human brain. 

As niches become more cutthroat and claims become more outlandish…

… prospects become hyper-aware of the wiles and tricks of marketing that they are almost immune.

It’s not an insurmountable obstacle…

… but it does leave anyone looking for shortcuts or any easy path to riches in the dust. 

Prospect resistance being at all-time highs also illustrates the importance of believability in marketing. 

Which, at risk of me sounding like a broken record stuck on an endless loop…

… brings us back to the value of LONG copy.

It can be a salesletter, VSL, webinar, or an in-person pitch.

The platform doesn’t matter, the message matters. 

You need space and time to explain ALL the benefits and establish yourself as the "go-to" authority.

And, this is important…

… long copy gives you plenty of space to give your prospect REASONS to justify their decision to the haters and mockers in their lives.

People fear mockery almost more than anything else in life. 

It’s why public speaking tops the list of number one fears for so many.

Your sales message doesn’t just need to explain all of the great benefits your prospect will soon be enjoying...

… you must also remove ALL fear of being suckered.

Your prospect should feel that they got the better end of the bargain. 

But even deeper: 

You know (because you’re an uber-sales-pro) that your prospect still won’t pull out their wallet if they catch a whiff of an easy way "out". 

You know that, even though they want your product or service desperately…

… and will KICK themselves for not buying right then and there…

… they won’t buy if they don’t feel any urgency to act now.

Even if not taking action goes completely against their self-interest. 

That’s the reason top marketers use anything in their arsenal to light a fire under their prospect’s butt:

* Limitations
* Deadlines
* One-time offers
* Bonuses
* Etc…

Once they leave without buying, the odds of them returning later are very, very, very low. 

So learn to sell already, would ya?
