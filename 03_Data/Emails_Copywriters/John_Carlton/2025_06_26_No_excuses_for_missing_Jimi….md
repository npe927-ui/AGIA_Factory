I learned the painful way so you don’t have to…Howdy.

Think of something you want to achieve, either in life or in biz. 

If you want it badly enough, you MUST make priorities and do your due diligence to follow through. 

It gets tough sometimes because there will ALWAYS be a competing reason not to do what needs to be done. 

Our brains work that way — always looking for a reason to be lazy.

Always looking for a reason to bail.

I know this from many painful experiences. So learn from my mistakes. 

Cuz here’s a whopper that I can’t believe I’m ‘fessing up to. 

19-year-old John almost got to see Jimi Hendrix. 

Almost. 

I knew the venue, had wheels, had friends to go with…

… heck, I even had the money, which was a freaking miracle that should have been interpreted as a sign from the heavens. 

But something came up and I had to miss it.  

Jimi died soon after. I never did see him perform.

You know what the worst part of this sad tale is?

I don’t even recall what seemed so damned important at the time. 

But you can bet that I remember missing the show. 

I know I talk about “no regrets.” But this is a big one.

The lesson is clear: 

Prioritize. 

Whatever it was that kept me away, I could have planned around it. 

* The girlfriend would have understood. 

* The family wouldn’t have missed me that much.

* I could have made it up to anyone who felt the least bit slighted by my absence.

Even if it was work-related, I could have rearranged things. 

There was NOTHING as important to me, at 19, as seeing Jimi.

Your mind will derail you; don’t let it happen. 

No one else can or will do the task for you.

In business, there will always be phone calls you really, really, really don’t wanna make or projects you really, really, really don’t wanna start… 

… or a whole pile of sundry details and major things that must be done that you would prefer not to do.

If a particular thing is routinely onerous to you, you should re-examine what you do for a living. 

Maybe you’re in the wrong gig.

But resistance happens even when you’re doing what you love. 

Every writer I know struggles to sit down and face the empty page. 

Much easier to go play Nintendo, or take a nap, text a friend, go eat something, or do ANYTHING else.

Discipline hurts, sometimes. Okay, it always hurts a little bit.

I love Tom Hanks’ line in “A League of Their Own”, when his star player says she’s quitting baseball because it “got too hard”. 

He says: “It’s SUPPOSED to be hard. That’s what makes it so special. If it was easy, everybody would do it.”

Keep that in mind when you need to hunker down and get past your resistance on something.

Life’s too short to miss out. 

Stay frosty, 

John
