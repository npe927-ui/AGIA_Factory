or is it just me?Howdy.

Have you noticed something disturbing going on with people lately?

No one seems to ever ask good questions anymore.

I’m not talking about just stirring up trouble. 

It really seems that people have lost their curiosity.

They stare at their phones like zombies, all while sitting an arm’s reach away from an actual fellow human being. 

When they do pipe up, they utter one word grunts or else babble away while saying nothing.

It’s rare now to ask anything meaningful when meeting new people or even when hanging out with close pals. 

Maybe I’m a dinosaur, but the lack of any questions at all, nevermind good ones, kind of irks me.

Maybe the cat finally killed curiosity, then.

Which is fine for civilians I suppose. 

But not for marketers.

We must always be awake and aware. 

Consider this:

The foundation of persuading prospects to buy is to know who you’re talking to. 

* What their fears and desires are…

* What’s important to them… 

* What’s keeping them up at night.

And you can’t find that stuff out by guessing.

You’ve got to dig deep into the mind of your prospect, do a helluva lot of research, and of course, ask GOOD questions, and LOTS of them.

Then you’ve got to listen and act on what you’ve learned.

But that’s another topic entirely.

Don’t even get me started.
