Top dogs vs the scaredy cats…Howdy.

Some biz owners refuse to do even slightly risky marketing, for fear of damaging their “reputations.”

Halbert often talked about this as “image suicide” — the necessity of killing and burying the “self” to move to a new level of success.

If you’re worried about what others might say about your use of bold advertising, then ask yourself:

What reputation?

Unless you’re the top dog in your niche, very few people (if anyone) gives a rat’s ass about what you think or do.

What these scaredy-cats are talking about when they say “reputation” isn’t how their market might respond…

… but what their family and friends think of them.

And this reeks of a losing attitude.

It sure ain’t Operation MoneySuck.

Success sometimes entails tough choices.

Think of the type of person you have to be to earn a million a year (if that’s your goal.)

Do you know what you need to do to make this real?

I’ve known many people who started the year with such a goal… who quickly modified it downward as the reality of the task became a burden.

Turns out they didn’t really want the whole million after all.

Half of that would suffice just fine.

Some people don’t have a clue how to earn that mil (or half).

So your goal really is: 

Figure out the type of person you need to become and what you need to do.

Your initial tasks are to ask, seek, and knock like crazy.

And change the way you move and act in the world.

Because you must transform yourself into the kind of person who earns that type of money.

Right now, maybe you aren’t that dude or dudette.

The transformation starts with changing your habits and decisions about how to spend your time.

So task “A” becomes sharpening your ability to craft a killer sales pitch while remodeling the kitchen gets pushed to the back burner.

Gradually, all of your priorities change.

It will be hard, sometimes, no doubt about it.

Especially when you discover your old gang no longer understands you or mocks your ambition.

They liked the old, non-threatening you. They want that version of you back.

But you’ve changed.

When you get hip to the glory of focused change, you never lament leaving the “old” you behind.

Hot new adventures are going to take up a lot more of your time now.

Stay frosty,
