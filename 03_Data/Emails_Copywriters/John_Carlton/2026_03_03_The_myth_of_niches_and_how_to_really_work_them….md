Striking the fine balance of immersing & getting the heck out of dodge…Howdy.

Ever hear the marketing cliche “there are riches in niches?”

True to a point, but like every seemingly simple principle, there are complexities. 

For example: remember that every niche market has its own mini-culture… 

… with its own lingo and its own history and its own code of honor.

Ignore these important details at your peril.

As a marketer, you gotta go deep into the world of your prospect.

I’m amazed that so many rookies are still trying to game the system.

They skip over the inconvenient research details and rush to send out ads that don’t have a chance of convincing anyone.

The cure is simple, too.

You gotta read what your audience reads and think as they think...

… yes, even if you disagree.

Connect your “insider” knowledge with broader knowledge of what’s going on, and you’ll be weighing your profits in buckets.

You must become part sociologist, part psychologist, and all-around voodoo salesman to find the passionate sweet spot of your prospect.

That’s why the best marketers read newspapers and magazines and watch popular culture television shows.

They are hip to what’s happening and can intelligently talk about it.

You can’t market in a vacuum.

But here’s the kicker:

You also can’t market when your brain has been turned to mush by too much popular culture.

Thus:  

You gotta dis-engage regularly, and get outa Dodge.

Especially when the inner world of your prospect turns into toxic, radioactive soup.

Let your grey matter recharge and soften up a bit.

Put the devices away and disconnect the Wifi.

You won’t die without it, I promise.

Also, let the dog decide where you’re gonna hike for a day.

When you get back, you’ll be refreshed, recharged, and ready to get back into the fray without sustaining too much brain damage.

Feeding your mind on killer ads that have worked in the past is a great way to get inspired.
