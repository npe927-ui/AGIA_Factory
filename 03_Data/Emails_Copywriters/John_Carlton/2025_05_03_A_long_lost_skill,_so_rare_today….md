Yet so necessary…Howdy. 

I took a lot of harsh criticism in my younger years, both from others and from myself, for not doing anything constructive with my life.

However, looking back, I see things differently now.

Yes, I was a slacker. 

But I was, surprisingly, honing a particular strange skill that has served me well over the years.

Turns out, I was good at ruminating. 

Which, at first glance, looks remarkably like slacking off. 

In reality it means noodling over difficult thoughts. 

Eventually, I would start to crave more success. 

But what I craved most of all… was time.

Time to read more books, listen to more music, indulge in more pleasure…

… and time to stare at the wall and go deep inside my own head. 

Yes, ruminating.

Silly me.

Here’s the cruel lesson my friend’s girlfriend delivered: 

One evening, she admitted she despised me…

… because I helped my buddy feel like he wasn’t alone with his wall-staring.

And it was high time that he moved beyond that “thinking crap”, and got busy building a life worthy of her Yupped-out aspirations.

I was stunned. 

“Thinking” was now a bad thing?

It was with Susie. 

She was whip-smart, and full of energy and life-force…

… but for her (and her ilk), the definition of “success” had nothing to do with having more “time” to spend staring at walls and pondering life.

Until then, I’d just assumed that was everybody’s dream.

And it scared the hell out of me to realize how many folks considered it a profound waste of time. 

And even highly distasteful, cuz it ruined the vibe when they wanted to discuss wine or stock market tips or country club memberships.

Yep. I was the shallow one.

I liked staring at the wall (or at the waves, or the clouds, or a blank piece of paper), and disappearing into my head for hours at at time.

And being able to do more of it seemed an excellent element of a “successful” life. 

You know, maybe like what Aristotle (or was it Socrates) said about “the examined life.”

Today, I’m more convinced than ever that it is THE main reason to succeed.

Because if you aren’t clear on WHY you want to get rich (or accomplish whatever goal you want)…

… then, even if you get there, you’re gonna be one lost puppy.

The “whys” of life are mostly ignored. 

It’s taken for granted that big houses, fancy sports cars, better looking spouses, bigger/better/nicer/more expensive everything is of COURSE the preferred goal.

And maybe that’s true for you.

I will tell you it is NOT true for the majority of friends and colleagues I’m closest to.

I’m closest to them because we are simpatico about what really matters in life.

And you don’t automatically figure out what matters, for you…

… unless you spend some serious time thinking about it. 

Pondering. Brooding. Daydreaming. Cogitating.

All critical skills if you want to be a top marketer and copywriter.

But the ruminating part is up to you. 

You can get what you want.

The thing is…

… WHY do you want it?

Something to think about at the start of a brand new month.
