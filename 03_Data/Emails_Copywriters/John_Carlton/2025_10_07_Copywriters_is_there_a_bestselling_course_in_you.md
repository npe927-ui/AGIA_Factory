A product that "sells itself" is a myth.

Lots of people fall for it and heartache follows.

If you followed me for any length of time, you'll know that one of my top sayings is that the marketing graveyard is full of fantastic, life-changing products…

… that customers would have loved…

… if only they had known.

Without killer marketing, even your best idea is dead in the water.

Now here’s the thing:

All smart marketers know where the real moolah is…

… and it’s NOT from one-off sales. 

It’s from repeat customers who are so thrilled that they want everything you put out from now on.

The more value you provide, the more they can’t wait to pounce on your next offer. 

For that, you really do need a killer product that overdelivers.

Then there’s a different hurdle.

Your customer actually has to USE your product…

… not file it somewhere in the dark recesses of their hard drive never to see the light of day. 

When selling a course, many copywriters immediately go from "marketing mode"  into "teacher mode."

In other words,they commit the worst deadly sin of them all:

They get boring.

Which isn't just the death of your copy.

It's the end. Period.

Your customer doesn’t get what they want.

And your relationship suffers for it. 

What's the answer?

As a copywriter, you already have the chops to keep readers on the edge of their seat.

Now just keep at it when delivering your course content.

Keep it snappy.

The customer may have already bought, but you are still selling.

Every chapter, every section, every module of your course has to continue to be the greased slide that compelled them to buy in the first place.
