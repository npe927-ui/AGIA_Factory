Natural born sales pros are rare. 

Successful marketers get there through years of training & experience… 

… but whether the skill is developed or inborn, these people intuitively understand how to channel killer selling chops when writing copy.

For most folks, the ability to get someone to buy remains a big damn mystery. 

This is particularly frustrating when you get your basic copywriting chops down — so your ad reads well, and covers all the bases… 

… and yet you don’t convert as many sales as you’d hoped for.

So here is the mystery, solved: 

Getting a prospect to say "Hey, that product seems cool" is EASY.

They may even agree with you that they SHOULD buy.

The tricky part is getting that same prospect to pull out their wallets and actually BUY.

This is where world-class salesmanship comes in. 

Even bad copywriters can coax a prospect to climb up and sit on the fence.

But it takes a deep knowledge of persuasion to knock them OFF that fence and into your yard as a paying customer.

I used to have to hide the fact I was teaching so much classic salesmanship… 

… because to many people, the whole concept seems fraught with scary implications of "mind control" and sleazy tricks.

The sooner you get past this hangup, the sooner you can start raking in the moolah.

Everyone sells. 

All the time. 

* Kids try to sell unrestricted access to the cookie jar to Mom
* Teens try to sell themselves as good dating material
* Every essay you ever wrote was a sales job for a good grade
* And every friend you have ever had was first  "sold" on liking you

Everything is better when you know how to sell.

If you’re in business, and you ignore this, you’re toast. 

You can create a fabulous product, or present a fabulous service… 

… and you can even get hordes of prospects to eagerly tell you how amazing you are, and how you should be filthy rich because everything you offer is so great.

But that’s just piling prospects up on the fence…

… where they will sit forever if you don’t learn how to knock ‘em off.

Success is about closing the deal.

It’s a lot like a relationship, from the first time you meet that person who knocks your socks off right through to the "I do."

Just one of the many reasons people who can sell lead better lives.
