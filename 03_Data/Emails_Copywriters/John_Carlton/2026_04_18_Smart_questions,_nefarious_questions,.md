 and the absolute worst questions of all… Howdy.

The art of asking good questions is imperative to achieving success, as a copywriter, freelancer, or anything else. 

So what are you too afraid to ask cuz you think you might look “dumb.” 

If you’re working with a coach, they’ve heard it. 

We’ve all been asked some pretty weird and outrageous things.

And if your coach is worth their salt, they don’t mind. 

There’s only one category of question that makes our eyes roll. 

More on that in a second. 

First, let’s talk about all the other types of inquiries we deal with on the regular. 

1. The very basics: 

* “How do I create products?”

* “What goes into a good ad?”

* “How do I execute a real marketing plan?”

These mostly come from rookies in biz. 

2. The second level of questioning comes from people who’ve dabbled in the marketing arts, but fall apart at the slightest obstacle. 

For example: 

"How do I stop procrastinating, overcome writer’s block (it’s a myth, by the way), and what do I do with these badly-written first drafts?"

3. The last level is from active marketers. 

And oddly enough, their questions often mirror the basic ones asked by rookies. 

Why is that?

The thing is you can have fabulous success with one project, and then hit a brick wall on the next.

Then the smart thing to do is to go back to the basics to see where you went wrong.

Now, these three levels of questions have their own subcategories. And here's the issue:  

There is no such thing as a “dumb” question. 

But like I said before, there ARE such things as “bad” questions. 

That’s when you know the answer but you ask anyway…

… because you don’t like the answer. 

Or the questions you ask because you think they make you look smarter than everyone else, but all you’re trying to do is stir up trouble. 

Bad faith questions, in other words. (That type really drives everyone insane. The absolute worst.)

Then there are the “stall” questions.

These are the ones that make people feel like they’re making progress. Cuz they’re asking questions, which means the gray cells are spinning, right?

But in reality they’re just putting off the tasks that actually get things done.

Great ads don’t get written by magic. 

You have to put the time in, starting with research. Tons of it, in fact. 

Then, and only then do the good ideas burble up from your well-fed subconscious. 

Now we come to the most nefarious type of question of all.

These are the ones people never ask because they feel they’re too… embarrassing. 

They’re often sticking points, too. You cannot go further without the answer.

So you gotta swallow your pride and ask away.
