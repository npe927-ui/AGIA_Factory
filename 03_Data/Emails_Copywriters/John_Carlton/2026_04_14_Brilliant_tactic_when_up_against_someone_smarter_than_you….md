This secret will help you win…Howdy.

When up against a competitor who is more skilled and has more experience than you, you’re doomed, right? 

Not necessarily. 

If you want to make it as a freelancer, you do need some serious chops. 

I would never say otherwise. 

So it makes sense to keep reading, learning, and implementing. 

But it might shock you that there’s something that matters just as much, possibly even more, that hardly anyone ever thinks about. 

I’m talking about your reputation for being trustworthy.

If you’re worried about your lack of experience thus far or that A.I. will take your job or suck away all your clients…

… then this should be one of your top areas of focus. 

(While lots of folks have gone all in on A.I, you gotta ask yourself, how much do they actually trust it?)

Do you believe the tech companies behind the scenes actually care about you?

I thought not. 

At the end of the day, very few people will say (or even think) to themselves, “yeah, I trust A.I with my life.” (The people who have fallen in love with their chatbots are the exception. But that’s beyond the scope of anything I can help with.) 

So if you want to get and stay successful in a world where the ground is shifting away under your feet…

… then become the copywriter and consultant that clients can TRUST completely. 

Nothing matters more. 

Keep their secrets. 

Avoid blabbing their juicy gossip to a few hundred of your closest pals.

Thanks to social media, confidential (and embarrassing) info can go viral in a matter of minutes.

But not by you. 

NEVER by you. 

A trustworthy pro is hard to find.

Clients want and need someone they trust who will handle their biz and info with all the care and attention they would if it were their own. 

So if freelancer A has more experience, but also a reputation for yapping…

… and you're perceived as someone who will keep top-secret info confidential…

… you will very often win in the end. 

One of the vows I made when I started my freelance career was to BE that guy you could trust. 

In an environment where untrue and business-killing rumors spread like wildfire...

… the ability to actually keep a secret was kinda empowering. 

When word got out (and it always does, good or bad), it helped me slip into inner circles and behind closed doors. 

My growing skills were a factor. 

But the fact that my network knew they were safe with me helped even more, even as I was a newbie coming up the ranks.

As social creatures, we have an almost overwhelming urge to share. 

But if you want to rise in your chosen profession and crave the excitement of being on the inside…

… then learn how to shut the hell up. 

It’s simple. 

It's also part of the job description when you start being a responsible, trustworthy dude or dudette.

REMEMBER: 

It takes a lifetime to build a reputation, and one slip-up to destroy it forever. 

Stay frosty,
