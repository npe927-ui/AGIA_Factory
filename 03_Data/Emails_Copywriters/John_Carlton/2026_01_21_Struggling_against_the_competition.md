Try this sneaky but ethical tip right now…Howdy.

The “Art of War” may have been written for ancient battles …

… but many of its core principles apply to the crazy biz world of today. 

Point number one is always to know thyself.

Point #2?

Know thine enemy more. 

For example:

Your competitors are smart. 

At least some of them. 

Others are the most vicious, viciously smart, conniving, super-savvy opponents on the planet..

Trying to stay one step ahead of them is impossible.

In some cases, the best way to win is to not even try.

I don’t engage in the neverending, exhausting battle of trying to keep up.

As a lazy dude who still managed to enjoy a massively successful career…

… I look for the short & simple ways to do anything.

(Like consulting with mentors, for instance.) https://m190.isrefer.com/go/swsaua2601/jcemail/

And so instead of fighting competitors, I cruise along in their wake to see if they’re onto anything I’m still clueless about.

And you should do the same.

I’m always appalled (appalled!) when I discover a client doesn't study the competition.

Ignore this virtual goldmine at your peril.

Now, biz is usually slightly less bloodthirsty than war. 

Usually.

But it's ridiculously easy to ethically and easily "spy" on your competitors.

Buy their products, and see how you’re treated by customer service. Give the stuff a trial run.

Hire them for whatever service they provide. 

Get a consultation. 

At the very least, start by just subscribing to their stuff.

Maybe this seems a bit sneaky.

But it’s all perfectly fair and legal…

… and it's the FIRST step you should take when starting a new biz or launching a new product. 

Knowing first-hand how your most successful competitors do things…

* how they treat prospects and customers

* how they process orders

* how their funnels develop from freebie to high-ticket, etc…

… you will never be blind-sided when things heat up.

The more you know, the better you can position your own bad self against them.

You can even make a point to offer what they lack, in service, product details, whatever. 

It’s a great way to develop your best possible "Unique Selling Position", because it’ll be drenched in the reality of what else is available out there for prospects.

I mean, dude, they’re spying on YOU…
