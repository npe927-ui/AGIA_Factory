Don’t let it sour your mojo…Howdy.

Excessive amounts of hype has always been a bone of contention for marketers. 

The constant screeching of "this is the BEST AND MOST AMAZING THING EVER" can actually sour people on products and opportunities with actual value.

"Hype", and the dreadful sales pitches filled with it gets really old, really fast.  

Even to the point where ethical marketers want to run to the opposite end of the scale.

They don’t want to be obnoxious or ruffle any feathers.

Hence the talk about "selling without selling".

Which also doesn’t work.

So then the promises and claims begin to get a little more bold, and then… 

… the pendulum swings back and the vicious cycle continues. 

The modern world seems as though it was designed to bug us all. 

None of our ancestors had to deal with virtual worlds or an economy based on blips and beeps. 

The "marketplace" of today is noisy, distracting, and crammed with scams and genuine opportunity in equal amounts.

Nothing about modern marketing is pretty.

* Having the best product means squat. 

* Being a genuinely nice person trying to do the right thing won’t save you from dwindling sales.

I have a saying that the marketing graveyard is FULL of fabulous products no one figured out how to sell.

Selling is the language of the marketplace–like it or not. 

Yeah, there’s too MUCH of it crammed into your inbox.

* It’s annoying

* It pisses us off

* And it makes our fight-or-flight urges kick in big time.

But hype, like beauty, is in the eye of the beholder.

What is hype to one person is a lifeline of information to someone else. 

If classic salesmanship offends you, you’re going to struggle to make your business work.

Every day, fresh new ideas are launched by good people who don’t believe they need sales knowledge to succeed…

… and those businesses are shuttered and empty soon after.

If you have an ethical product that your prospect really should have (because it will make his life better, or fix a dire problem he has)…

… then SHAME on you if you ignore the proven ways to get his attention… and fail to sell him on it just because you’re uncomfortable with the idea of selling.

So here are some principles to clear things up and get you on the right path:

* Selling does NOT mean being obnoxious.

* Selling does NOT require you to be someone you’re not.

Introverts can learn to sell, without any hype at all. 

Anyone can learn when they follow a proven system. 

Just promise me you’ll never make the mistake of thinking you can "sell without selling." 

That nonsense will bankrupt you.
