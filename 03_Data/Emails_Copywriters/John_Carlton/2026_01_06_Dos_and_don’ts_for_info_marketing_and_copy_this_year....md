Here it is, 2026, and as always, I am once again hearing the rumor that information marketing is dead. 

Rest in Peace.

We hardly got a chance to know you, and you will be missed. 

But is it really dead and gone...

... or is it like a Marvel hero that bounces back in a different reincarnation, stronger than ever? 

Anyone currently making a living by selling how-to courses is sure to be very surprised at the so-called demise of info marketing. 

Mind you, the biz model has been abused relentlessly.

Thanks to the pervasiveness of AI slop, the situation is even worse.

If you can imagine it, there’s a course for it. Probably dozens, in fact. 

But here’s both the good news and bad news for anyone dreaming of getting their ideas off the ground…

… prospects can tell when they’re no longer getting value, and they feel it viscerally when they’re no longer cared about. 

If you’re gonna sell courses, you’ve got to make sure they deliver on ALL promises and then some. 

And don’t forget about the most important part:

How are you going to promote the thing?

In 99% of the advertising out there…

… the copy just plain sucks.

It’s so boring it hurts.

This short list of do’s and don’ts will get you started. 

Do: Learn to write with a little outrageous flair.

Don’t: Try to outshout or outwork the competition…

Do: Outsmart them.

Do: Write copy that startles your reader, wakes him up and lingers inside his head.

Don’t: Be boring. That’s a mortal marketing sin.

Do: Be memorable. 

Tell a story that intrigues your reader no end, and hopefully wakes ‘em up in the middle of the night to ponder your offer.

Have you ever heard of that concept "message to market match?" 

Nowhere is this more critical than in info marketing. 

Better-than-average copy is the life-blood of your biz.

Those days of slapping something together and expecting folks to fight and claw for the front of the line are long gone (if they were ever here to begin with.)

And you can’t rely on a couple prompts and get AI to do all the work for you either.

Though it can help with the grunt work...

... you still have to know your prospects, hopes, dreams, fears, and deepest wishes. 

In a crowded market, you gotta compete fiercely for prospects’ ever-shrinking attention span and be waayyyy more savvy than your competitors. 

If you can figure out how to crawl inside your reader’s brain before they do, you’ll be ahead of the game.
