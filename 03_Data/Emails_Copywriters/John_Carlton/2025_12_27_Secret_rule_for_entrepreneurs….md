Rule #1: 

Decision Moments define your future.

It can happen gently or like a sledgehammer. 

Your choice. (See what I did there?)

There will be numerous moments where you HAVE to make a decision.

To get all scaredy-cat and avoid making a decision actually IS a decision. 

Usually, the worst one possible.

But a decision, nonetheless.

Alas, having to make a choice turns many us humans into quivering blobs of jello.

The solution: 

Don’t freak out. 

A bad decision is reversible 99.99% of the time. 

Rule # 2: 

Do NOT "eeny miney moe."

You don’t just say "Oh, what the hell. Let’s go with door number 3. What could possibly go wrong?"

Instead, prepare your butt for what comes AFTER making the decision. 

For example: 

If you think you’d like to go live in BoozeTown cuz it’s got a beach and seems fun, go vacation there first. 

Don’t just pack up and move.

Following mindless intuition is one of the MOST COMMON blunders I see.

Hunches are okay for the early stages of approaching a decision. 

But run those hunches through the Reality Grinder before acting on them.

Think up some possible scenarios. 

* Scenario one: "It worked!", and that’s it. Happy days.

* Second scenario: "It failed miserably."

* Scenario three: The results are inconclusive. (Yep, that happens a lot, too.)

Always have a Plan B cooking. 

There is no real failure, cuz you’re just testing. 

Campaigns and engagements can and will tank. 

It just means you need to figure out your next step.

Which means more decisions to make.

* Like testing more

* Getting more info from research and your network

* Or trying a different tactic to jar loose more definitive results

Such is the essence of being an entrepreneur. 

Home runs, followed by draughts, followed by bunts and errors and strike outs. And rain outs. 

And then your car gets a flat in the parking lot.

Your ability to make a decision based on good input, solid research and being ready for what happens next puts you into the Elite World of great entrepreneurs.

Now, go make a decision, already and stop goofing around.

Even if it is just about what party hat to wear on Wednesday night.
