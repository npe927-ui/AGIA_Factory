This is all that matters… Howdy.

People are, almost universally, dead wrong about what makes an ad “good.”

Even pro-level marketers sometimes fall into this flawed way of thinking. 

I don’t normally go off on there being ONLY one way to do something. 

But this is the exception:

Because there really is only ONE way to judge the quality of any ad: 

Does it WORK?

Not, does it entertain? 

Not, is it inoffensive in every conceivable way, so no one gets riled up? 

Above all, "Does your spouse like it"  is NO way to judge an ad.

But people who should know better still do it.

All the time.

If you are a rookie in business, please take this one piece of advice from a grizzled veteran: 

Be VERY careful who you take advice from.

You can gather two dozen of your closest, most trusted friends, and ask them for advice on how to market your biz. 

Their hearts will be in the right place, they will be sincere, and many will honestly believe they can tell you exactly what to do and what to avoid.

And, if none of your friends has any actual experience in marketing… 

… you can BET all that wonderful advice will be somewhere around 100% wrong.

World-class salesmanship is a very non-intuitive set of learned skills on par with learning to play a musical instrument. 

It’s not part of the original equipment issued when you come into this world.

Fortunately, you probably don’t NEED to be at a world class level of salesmanship to succeed in biz.

But you need to at least learn the basics. 

Here are two principles to get you started:

1) You will never change someone’s mind just because you have facts and results on your side.
2) People will stubbornly cling to a welded-in belief even when it clearly is hurting them. 

Before I learned to parse out the most oblivious clients as a freelancer, I was frequently faced with biz owners who would interfere with a winning ad because their spouse “had a better idea.”

Then they’d refuse to admit they’d made a mistake even as their profits plummeted.

Silly humans, right?

This is the kind of thing you gotta learn to rise above. 

Stay frosty,

John

P.S. The illogical nature of the human mind is precisely why high-end salesmanship causes such outrage among the clueless.
