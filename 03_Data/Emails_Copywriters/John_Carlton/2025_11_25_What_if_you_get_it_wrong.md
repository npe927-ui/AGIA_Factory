The horror…Howdy.

Some people can never be entrepreneurs or marketers for a glaringly simple reason:

Because the path to success involves making decisions, and that causes instant paralysis. 

The worst part about all this?

Early decisions don’t even matter that much. 

Sure, there can be consequences to taking the wrong path. 

But they are rarely as dire as you make them out to be. 

Plus, each movement comes with a host of valuable lessons that you would never get by standing still. 

Maybe that doesn’t sound anything like what you hear in school or what armchair preachers proclaim. 

To hear some folks talk, you’d think any decision is irreversible and will have an eternal impact on your destiny.

But smart entrepreneurs know you can backtrack on any choice you’ve encountered.

If you don’t like where things are headed, just go back and take the OTHER road. 

* You can change horses mid-stream.

* Abandon failing efforts.

* Even plow through the wilderness with no idea what you’re doing to search for another road entirely.

Even after your flight has taken off and you’re already in mid-air – you can adjust. 

In fact, this is how MOST "real life" adventures proceed. 

Along the way, you’ll face periods of chaos and of pure joy.

There are multiple moments where everything changes.

A sudden epiphany ends one adventure and immediately kick-starts another.

Take any successful entrepreneur, and map out the path they took to get to that successful place. 

I don’t care WHO you pick.

Their path to achievement will NEVER be a straight line.

No one gets a clean trip without problems or disasters or even major failures along the way.

And this is why successful entrepreneurs run a little differently from the rest of the population.

They work without a safety net…

… but NOT because they’re crazy or love taking risks.

They just understand that they can always go back and start again any time.
