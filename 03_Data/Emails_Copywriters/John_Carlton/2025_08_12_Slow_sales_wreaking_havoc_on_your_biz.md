Howdy.

Slow sales can strike cold-blooded terror into the heart of any biz owner.

My best advice?

* First, calm down. 

* Take a deep breath. 

* Now go have a nice cold drink.

Some factors are going to be beyond your control.

Few biz owners have any influence on the world events that cause everything to flip upside down. 

Even when things are business as usual, there are, and always have been, “dead spots” in most markets. 

No matter what anyone tells you.

Certain seasons are just a black hole. 

But that doesn’t mean you give up.

No, no, no.

You are a mover and shaker.

Giving up is NOT in your Bag O’ Tricks.

Instead, study to understand your market on an even deeper level. 

With a little focus and effort, you can track the buying patterns of your flock. 

For example: 

“Common sense” suggests the best time to send out golf-related pitches is in the Spring when everyone starts dragging their clubs out, right?

Nope. 

NEVER listen to common sense.

It only leads you astray.

See, the best time for that niche is actually during the bone-chilling months of winter… 

… cuz all the golf nuts are just dreaming of that first warm round of the season. 

Markets differ in their patterns.

August is “too hot” for some niches.

Soon afterward, the “too cold” ones will be on fire once again.

So if your response takes a dive…

... try not to panic. 

Instead, stick your head outside and see what’s up.

Maybe there’s something out-of-the ordinary happening. 

Or it could be a typical seasonal up & down. 

It really doesn’t matter. 

What’s important is that you use any slow periods to spend some quality time with your list. 

And every once in a while, it pays off huge to just contact your best customers… 

… and GIVE them something.

For free.

No strings attached.

It’s part of the bonding process. 

Like any relationship, it’s nice to bestow a generous, unexpected gift every once in a while.
