Success in anything requires a delicate balance.

This is true whether as a marketer, biz owner, freelancer or anything.

You hear me rail about perfectionism often.

It’s a fair lesson that bears relentless repetition…

… because some people NEVER get anywhere because they NEVER stop tinkering.

It’s also bad to be WAY too lax about the stuff you’re willing to hit the “send button” on.

Don’t get me wrong.

No one would ever label me a perfectionist.

I’m messy and generally lazy. 

Except when it comes to writing and marketing.

Then, out of nowhere, I turn into Mr. Type A Obsessive-Compulsive.

I’m merciless about making things work.

Because a great sales pitch depends on clarity. 

I never  insist on PERFECTION.

But to be “good enough”...

… it MUST be clear. 

Many writers clog up their message with obtuse metaphors and clunky anecdotes. 

So here are some ground rules to help you strike that healthy balance between perfectionism and slovenliness. 

Rule Number One: There is NEVER room for a tangent. 

A good sales pitch is a greased slide. 

Rule Number 2: Weak writing won’t cut it.

That’s why my most advice is to rewrite more. 

Edit more viciously. 

In the upper echelons, it is not uncommon for top writers to craft a hundred headlines before choosing the best one. 

Personally, I love to edit.

It’s like polishing a statue I’ve just chiseled. 

Writing is communicating.

It is not merely typing words out in succession.

If your copy isn’t working the way you hope…

… it’s probably a matter of spending more time editing and rewriting. 

Sweat over your words a little bit. 

Just not so much that your “good enough” copy never sees the light of day.
