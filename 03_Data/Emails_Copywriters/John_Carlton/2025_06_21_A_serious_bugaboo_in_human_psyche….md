Laugh your way past the critics…Howdy. 

In almost every human endeavor, pleasing “most people” means downgrading the quality to abysmal levels.

Whether you’re selling a product, honing your athletic skills, or bringing your music to the world, there will be an absurd number of critics and cynics telling you how to “do or be better.”

The best advice is to pay them no regard and keep mastering your craft. 

Case in point: 

I once read a smug review of a Dire Straits concert by a reporter who wrote that Mark Knopfler was “never very good at playing the guitar”. 

Uh, okay. 

Are we talking about the same Mark Knopfler?

As in the dude who was inducted into the Rock Hall of Fame…

… and whose jaw-dropping masterpiece “Sultans of Swing” has been cited by other guitar greats as probably the best solo ever performed using a Fender Strat?

Then there are the reporters who act as though Olympic athletes are egomaniacs who will accept nothing less than the gold medal. 

The silver or bronze is a humiliating insult.

One commentator said, in reference to an aging ice skating star who choked during her event and had to “SETTLE” for the bronze: 

“I cannot imagine what she will do with the rest of her life, now that her dream of Olympic gold has been shattered.”

I have this bugaboo with people who make their living or take obscene amounts of pleasure in ripping others to shreds. 

You know the type: 

Music critics who have never attempted to master an instrument. 

Film reviewers who have zero acting experience. 

And, on a more personal level, biz gurus who have no life experience dealing with people or have never actually run a functional operation. 

Critics in all fields never experience the kind of dedication that drives someone to excel. 

(Hint: It’s the love of their craft and the pursuit of excellence - not the medals or trophies or adulation from the crowd.) 

A critic believes they can dabble around the edges of expertise, and that gives them the right to viciously judge everyone else for the slightest misstep.

Do you know what really happens when a high achiever fails on their way to their “dream” goal?

I’ll tell you, because I happen to know – and I have the bumps and bruises to prove it.

A true pro picks themselves up and dust themselves off. 

They look for the next logical step and they keep on keeping on.

Most importantly, they ignore all of the yappers and the naysayers gloating in their momentary failures. 

Developing a thick skin like this is a tall order, but it’s another essential skill as you rise up through the ranks. 

It takes a very disciplined dedication to achieve great results. 

No one hits a home run the first time they pick up a bat.

So keep at it. 

And stay frosty,
