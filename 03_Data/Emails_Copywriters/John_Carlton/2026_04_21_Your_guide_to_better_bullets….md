Today, we dive deep into the hidden psychology of making your subheads, bullets, and fascinations shiny and irresistible. 

Remember that your sales message may be mass delivered to thousands of prospects...

... but every single person reading it should feel as though you’re directing it to them personally:

* Men

* Women

* Introverts

* Extroverts

* People with children

* People without children

* Single people who wish they were married

* Married people who wish they were single

I’m generalizing here, of course, but the list goes on and on and on.

Often, copywriting gurus will tell you that you need a completely different pitch for every variation of audience segment. 

But who has time for that?

With good bullets and subheads, this extra work becomes unnecessary.

However: you DO need to be aware of what motivates each group so that your message:

* Answers their questions about what this product is, does, and how to order

* Eliminates any objections 

* Provides the sense that they’re getting the deal of the century  

Enter the concept of bullets.

Basic copywriting books will tell you to write a long list of dozens or even hundreds of one-liners, but they seldom explain why.

(Partly, it’s a design thing. Snappy short sentences have a way of drawing the eye.) 

But it goes much deeper than that. The real reason:  

Your list of bullets is where you get the chance to cover the reasons-to-buy that may not be so evident elsewhere in your copy.  

Also, the reasons NOT to buy.

Think of the privacy issues of ordering certain types of products, for example.  

Imagine a man receiving a large box on the front porch announcing that the "How to Be a Better Lover" book he ordered is inside. 

Just the thought of his wife, kids, neighbours, or even the drive-by porch pirate seeing it is enough to make him die of embarrassment. 

______________________________________________________________________

It’s enough to make him think twice about ordering that book in the first place…

… UNLESS:

You reassure him in no uncertain terms that the product will arrive in a plain, unmarked box.
______________________________________________________________________

Then there are those kids, glued 24/7 to their devices, who will recoil in terror at the thought of calling the toll-free number on your website, but will think nothing of engaging with the chatbot or sending a text.

That’s their language. 

Personalized bullets call out to specific segments of your audience without alienating the others.

* For extroverts, bold statements that make them the star of the story resonate.

* For introverts, the existence of a SINGLE bullet addressing their specific concerns can stand out like a match igniting on the page.

If they hit the mark, you obliterate all objections a prospect may have about ordering: 

* It’s too expensive

* There’s no time

* It won’t work anyway

* I’ve tried stuff like this before 

* This product is for someone else, not me

* I’m too embarrassed to order

* Etc 

You’ve got to think of everything that all of your prospects might be thinking as they consider your offer.

Otherwise, you might fail to mention the biggest benefit of that product to that particular buyer.

It gets even more convoluted when you realize, that, in spite of the modern tendency to avoid social interaction…

… every human actually longs for meaningful connections with other humans. 

Introverts crave a sense of "belonging", too… 

… as long as they don’t have to pretend to be extroverts. 

What a weird, twisted, fascinating species we all are. 

Anyway, now you understand the reason for so many bullets. 

You can’t leave anything – or anyone– out. 

Think of everyone who might be interested in your product, and list out:

* The reasons they would buy

* The reasons they would hesitate

* All of the ways their life will improve after they order (Remember, this will vary for each segment. And you know what that means… … yes, write more bullets.)

Different people, with different motivations, individual fears, and unique, lofty goals that they don’t dare to dream, but they dream anyway. 

That’s what you’re working with here, folks. 

Understanding this subtle psychology can really boost your bottom line.
