Fundamental biz rule # 1 Howdy.

Here is one of the first fundamental rules of doing biz: 

If you’re ashamed of anything you’re doing, then STOP doing it. Right now.

It’s that simple on paper. Not always easy to implement in real life. 

But critically important to your long term success and ability to sleep at night. 

A good product or service is NO guarantee you’ll succeed. 

You still need a solid biz and marketing plan. 

But when you DO have a good product, you can be bold and aggressive with your marketing with zero shame…

… knowing you’re performing a service to your fellow humans, providing value, and supporting our shaky civilization and economy. 

As a young punk coming up through the ranks, I couldn’t understand why anyone would CHOOSE to sell crap when there was no inherent reward for doing so.

The marketplace is full of excellent, life-changing products. 

And it takes just as much marketing and salesmanship to sell garbage as it does to sell quality. 

And yet, the world remains crammed with snake oil and BS and scams.

Turns out, there’s a reason for this. 

The biz world is not black-and-white, but very nuanced, which is a royal pain in the ass. 

It would be so cool if there were just good people, and bad people, and you got to choose freely who you worked with (or lived amongst).

But that ain’t the case.

And the worst of the bad actors LOOK for sleepy, low-information types to take advantage of. 

If they catch you in their sights, they’re happy, and you’re meat.

So here are the 3 main categories I suggest you get familiar with and learn how to avoid whenever possible. 

Cynics: 
These are the least harmful category, but very annoying. Cynics usually produce nothing, provide no value, and exist only to tear things down. 

Sociopaths:
To this type, other people are just like furniture — useful at times, and nothing to get attached to. If you get hurt, it’s no big deal unless there are also consequences for the sociopath. 

Assholes: 
This category is tough. The Azzhole Scale is so variable that it’s almost impossible to nail down. A “good” kind of prick will say mean things to you, but are even  harder on themselves. And some of the azzholes out there really are despicable, evil bastards to avoid at all costs.

If we really lived in a black-and-white world, all cynics, sociopaths, and azzholes would be easy to spot from a mile away. 

Unfortunately, this isn’t the case.

Reality is a mixed bag and you need to stay aware your entire life.

Also, don’t be quick to judge others using flawed standards. 

One man’s jerkiness is often another man’s genius. 

We all need to learn to see beyond the obvious.
