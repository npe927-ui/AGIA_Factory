You're smarter than you think if you know this trick...Howdy. 

The ability to deconstruct ideas and sales messages is THE main tool in any good marketer’s kit.

Start by actively re-examining everything you read and hear.  

This includes news articles, data, info-rich books, emails…

…  all of it.

Here’s a simple trick: 

Try to put anything that you aren’t “getting” into your own words. 

You can do this in your head, but writing it down is far better.

Common phrases will go in one ear and out the other.

But if you reconstruct them, you (and anyone listening to you) might just sit up and take notice. 

For example, I often teach people to “critical think.”

Not critical thinking. 

A simple tweak to the language is like a big STOP sign for your brain.  

Try it, next time you’re reading something you feel might be important.  

Reconstruct the concepts, sentences, and ideas into new language.

Consider how the concept might be interpreted in street slang, or translated for an 8-year-old.

Stop treating your brain as though it’s a passive “intake bucket” for any garbage you read or hear.

So learn to examine stuff to the point that you can rephrase it without losing the meaning.

Even if it’s nonsensical. 

Or especially if it’s nonsensical. 

Part of being awake is to take your ideas out for a walk in the cold, cynical world every now and then, and invite pot-shots.  

See if the little buggers can withstand scrutiny and abuse.

But have fun with it. 

A little laughter with your lessons also helps to make ‘em stick. 

Fun fact: you’ll remember bizarre phrases better, because they’re strangely memorable.  

The first poem I ever learned, which I still remember, was from a Roger Miller song: “Roses are red, and violets are purple, sugar is sweet and so is maple surple.”  

Memorable, right?

Now it’s firmly cemented in your brain too.

You’re welcome.
