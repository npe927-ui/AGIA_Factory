Here are some words to live by for this weekend and to take with you in the coming week:

No one has life – or biz – “all figured out.”

No one. 

And even when we think we have a handle on it…

… everything changes.

Life’s just like that.

We all drop balls, all the time.

This is something to keep in mind when you’re searching for folks to trust...

… colleagues, partners, clients, employees, spouses, whatever…

And yet, we seem to crave those mystical gurus who confidently claim to have the “hidden” secret.

It's a major kink in our human operating system (which comes with no manual, by the way)…

... that allows us to believe that someone out there has got this existence thang nailed.

Then reality intrudes and you realize that what you thought was competence…

... was really just the product of hiding from your consciousness all the uncomfortable crap you hate dealing with.

We're all juggling a whacky mess of emotional, intellectual, physical and metaphysical balls every moment we breathe. 

It's baked into life.

If you crave leadership, look for reluctant leaders — those few who simply make themselves available.

The loud-mouthed ones elbowing their way onto your radar, bragging up a storm, must be viewed with cautious alarm. 

Avoid them at all costs.

The mentors you want are the dudes or dudettes who've been around the block a few times, know the game well, and have succeeded at it. 

Not with smoke and mirrors, but with a great reputation and hard work and a commitment to doing the right thing at all times.

These types of people are shockingly rare, but they are out there. 

Life would be easier if everyone was who they insist they are.

Don't buy into that myth. You'll get burned time and time again.

Just a word to the wise.
