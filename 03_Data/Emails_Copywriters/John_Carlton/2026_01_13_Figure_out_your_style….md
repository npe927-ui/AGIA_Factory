Critical for all copywriters & marketers…Howdy.

Have you ever figured out your own personal learning style?

We all have one, you know. 

It is ABSURD to think there is any single way to teach anything to everyone.

There may be a finite number of ways to teach effectively… but there are certainly more than a tidy few.

This was one of the main reasons I got into teaching entrepreneurs. 

I noticed, way back when I first started producing marketing and copywriting http://john-carlton.com/marketing-tips programs, that there were always a few people in the audience who simply didn’t “get it” no matter what I did or said. 

I soon discovered that those non-learners fell into certain categories: 

* Some of these people were just willfully stupid. Not cuz they weren’t capable of understanding, they just refused to.

* Others could only “value” what I taught through their need to control the message. They were offended by any show of cockiness or humor.

* Some were aghast by the lack of democratic input I would allow into the conversation. No one who insists on wasting my time arguing about whether anyone reads long copy or not gets equal time with untested BS.

At first, I felt like a failure for not somehow possessing a super-broad array of teaching skills that would conquer any barrier.

Then I got over it.

I stumbled through my own educational days mostly clueless about everything. 

So I grew up thinking there was something wrong with ME.

First, because I couldn’t find the value in what was being shoved down my throat at school.

Then later, because I kept encountering people who insisted they wanted to learn what I had to teach about marketing and advertising and copywriting… but couldn’t seem to understand me at all.

And then, when I finally looked at the problem in a different way, it dawned on me: 

I was amazingly effective as a teacher… 

… with the right student.

Those who quickly pick up marketing prowess have several personality traits going for them:

* They can put their ego aside. 

* They have an open mind about new ideas, even when these ideas contradicted prior belief systems. 

* They have the ability to “translate” what they are learning into language that works for them.

I finally realized that it was pointless to pay ANY attention to those good folks who didn’t “click” with me.

Because those people I DID click with stood to benefit enormously.

This is why my first course was titled “Kick-Ass Copywriting Secrets of a Marketing Rebel https://m190.isrefer.com/go/kacs/jcemail/”. 

To this day, I still get outraged emails from people who are deeply insulted by the term “kick-ass”. 

Many Puritans demand that I change it immediately. 

All decree that they would NEVER deal with a teacher like me, as long as I used such foul language.

What they DON’T realize is that I used the term “kick-ass” deliberately.

It’s a big damn cleaver to separate those folks who will NEVER learn from me in any meaningful way from those who are PERFECT students for my teaching style.

I can’t pretend to be someone I’m not and be an effective teacher. 

I’m never gonna wear a tie, or go to bed at a decent time so I can get up early, or create an academically-approved syllabus for anything. 

But I stand behind my balls-to-the-wall, street-savvy teaching method because I’ve got a loooooong line of people willing to testify that my style worked with them, where others failed. 

There’s often a brief jolt of pain from my methods.

But stepping into the world of honest salesmanship and wicked-good copy is invigorating, NOT scary.

And for the right student — a savvy, independently-minded realist, who embraces the world for what it is, and doesn’t waste time wishing it was something else — this re-birth can be the start of an adventure that changes everything. 

Forever. 

In your business, and in your life.

The world is big, filled with people who are wired with all kinds of passions that — when you’re playing it smart — fit into your sales funnel perfectly. 

You just gotta know what to say and when https://m190.isrefer.com/go/kacs/jcemail/.
