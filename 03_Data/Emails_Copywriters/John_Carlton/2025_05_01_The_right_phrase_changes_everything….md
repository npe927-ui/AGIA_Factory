Get punched or make a sale...Howdy.

The almost magical power of words never fails to amaze me.

In marketing and copywriting, the right phrase can make you a fortune.

In person, they can help you NOT get punched in the face. 

Or GET you punched in the face if you’re not careful. 

Like this scenario I talked myself out of (after first getting myself into it in the first place.)

Here’s what happened. 

While leaving the plane at an airport, I noticed that the guy ahead had a hole in his back pocket and his wallet was about to fall out. 

So I tell the guy as I pass, "Dude, you’re about to lose your wallet."

He looks at me in confusion.  

Then quickly puts a hand on his wallet, which was still there, hanging by a thread.

Then glared a gaze of pure suspicion and budding rage back at me.

Understandable.

Talking about someone losing their wallet tends to provoke an immediate WTF reaction - especially at an airport when tensions are already high.

Was I about to rob him?

And what was I doing looking at his ass anyway?

Was I some kind of sick creepo?

The scene was about to get ugly, but then I said 5 words that calmed the situation completely.

"Your back pocket’s split open," I said.  

That simple explanation revealed that I wasn’t a gloating thief.

Suddenly, he was all thankful and apologetic.

He instantly went from wanting to punch me to feeling like I had done him a huge favor.
.
The difference between him thinking I was up to something no good… and that I was a good guy just trying to help?

Two seconds worth of communication.

Instantly, I went from creepo to hero.

I managed to avoid fisticuffs and turn a bad situation good in a blazing hurry.

All thanks to a couple of well chosen words. 

Something to consider as you make your sales messages lean and mean.
