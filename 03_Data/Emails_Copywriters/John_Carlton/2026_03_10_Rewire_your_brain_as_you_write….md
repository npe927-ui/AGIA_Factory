Howdy.

Something unique happens in your brain when you write. 

You take intangible language and translate it into physical form. 

Sometimes, you write out concepts even before they take shape in the language center of your cerebral cortex, skipping all internal dialogue. 

The written manuscript becomes the only way the concept is expressed.

Non-writers really can’t wrap their head around this concept.

And while all writers love to produce good stuff, the process can be a tad daunting over the long haul. 

So here’s my tip of the day. 

I’m sure I’ve written about this before…

… but I also often hear professional writers admit they constantly forget this valuable tidbit. 

So I repeat: 

Set aside time each day dedicated to writing.

Make it the same time each day, too, so it becomes a habit. 

Give that time TOP priority in your life — shut off all phones, lock the door...

... and if you have to, get nasty with people who insist on violating your time. 

When people close to you finally realize you’re serious about your dedicated alone time, they will grudgingly accept it… 

… eventually they’ll even help you in protecting it. 

They might also mock you, because this kind of discipline is seen as suspect in our slacker culture.

But they’ll respect you for doing it just the same.

Top writers never wait for “inspiration” to arrive.

And they never fear “writer’s block” or allow it to hold them back. 

“Inspiration” and “writer’s block” are both fictional ideas for true professionals. 

Set aside a certain time to write and follow it.

Your brain will get the message soon enough.

I used to even have “writing clothes” — filthy sweats and even a battered hat I wore as a “uniform” to announce to myself and the world that I was, by God, a writer who was ready to write. 

Now go figure out a winning routine (and maybe uniform) that works for you. 

Stay frosty,

John
