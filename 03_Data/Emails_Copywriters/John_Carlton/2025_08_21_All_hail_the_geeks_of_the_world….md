Someone has to save us…Howdy.

Geeks are gonna take over the world one day…

…. which is a good thing, cuz someone has to save us all from ourselves. 

And for the most part, the nerdy folk are the good guys. 

Smart as hell, and they’re having a blast in this Brave New Wired World.

I love hanging out with them.

Plus, they’re the ones who are making it easy for guys like me to come along.  

I’ve never tried to program software, don’t know a thing about code, and would be just as lost now in the AI basement…

… if all the tech geniuses hadn’t pushed so hard for friendly interfaces and user-centered computing.

Big corporate types are just fine with keeping new technology away from the masses.  

If The Man had his way, we might still be on dial-up modems, with streaming video reserved for the wealthy and the military.  

And the only way to get music would be on CDs. (Okay, so it wouldn’t be all bad.)

You can pretend that the entrepreneur honchoing new stuff is merely an interchangeable cog in the wheel of invention.  

That the light bulb, car, radio, television, space flight, Internet, personal computer and every other gew-gah supporting modern life would have been invented anyway…

Maybe later, maybe in some slightly different form, it would all be here somehow at some point…

… but anyone with a smidgeon of knowledge about the history of civilization can refute that idea.  

The Internet could have remained a pet project of the military-industrial-academic world.  

The sky could today be full of zeppelins instead of jets, with no satellites orbiting above, no footprints on the moon, and no music or podcasts murmuring directly into your earbuds through wireless headphones.

Humans have a built-in drive to tinker with stuff, to make swords out of ploughs…

… to increase comfort and hide unpleasantness, to be curiouser and curiouser about things that blow up and move mountains and open minds.

There isn’t one path to take, at any time.  

Mobs resist change.

King-makers subvert progress.

And corporations don’t like crazy guys messing with the bottom line.

Think of the world we live in today… 

… with the Internet woven completely into daily existence, with once-dominant industries crippled even as brand new entrepreneurial opportunities bloom.
