Howdy.

Too many people love to show off.

(It's basically an epidemic in the biz world.) 

Either out of ego or necessity, nearly everyone is scrambling to be perceived as  "the smartest dude or dudette in the room".

This attitude isn't just annoying. 

It can be outright dangerous.

But NEVER
underestimate the real wisdom in a simplistic worldview.

Mensa-type folks can be clueless doofus-morons when theory is left behind and we must start moving in the real world.

And vice versa.

Many who earned poor grades in school can conquer their chosen market. 

Whereas MBA graduates with honors couldn't sell to save their lives. 

And here's what the humble folk understand that "geniuses" can NOT wrap their heads around:

Being brilliant in one area of life does not equate to competence in anything else. 

People who are smart in their profession can be idiots in all other aspects.

Seriously: 

* Surgeons who are kicking butt against disease are often patsies for financial scams.  

* Wildly successful biz owners get their heads handed to them when they boldly venture outside their area of expertise.  

* Sports heroes can't keep a decent relationship together.

When someone thinks they can succeed at "Y" because they've succeeded at "X"...

... they are usually
on the fast train to nowhere.

It took me years to figure this out.

When you're trying to judge whether someone deserves your attention or not, it helps to discern what actual knowledge they have (as opposed to an abundance of hot air.)

Here's the breakdown of what I've learned to look for:

* Intellectual Credibility Factor #1: Some people are blessed with raw, natural intelligence. (Lucky bastards.)

* Intellectual Credibility Factor #2: Some people gorge on education.

* Intellectual Credibility Factor #3: Others possess street-level savvy from their time in the trenches.

In other words, a combination of:

* Intelligence.  

* Education.  

* Savvy.

Having all three attributes won't guarantee success, mind you. 

But it sure can turn the tides in your favor.
