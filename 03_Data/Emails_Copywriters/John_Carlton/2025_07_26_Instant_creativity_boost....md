So simple, yet so underestimated... Howdy.

Everyone of us has a phenomenal creativity and problem solving tool readily available. 

We just keep forgetting to use it. 

All you have to do is “let the unconscious work it out.” 

This tool has been around for eons and used by some of the most brilliant minds in history. 

In advertising, the great David Ogilvy would assign his unconscious the task of arriving at a solution before going to sleep. 

Here’s how I do it:

First, I cram my head with all the info and tidbits I can find.

Then I turn it all off and take a nap or a walk or engage in a hobby.

This is also called “getting cold” on the material.

When I return to my desk, I’ll have multiple headline ideas flooding my consciousness as soon as I hit the keyboard.

The headline that bubbles up may or may not be the one that makes it to the final draft.

That doesn’t matter.

The hard work of sorting through the vast amounts of info has been done, and clarity ensues.  

And you have a fresh view of things, which is impossible when you’re down in the trenches of stress.

It’s much better than beating your head against the wall.

Stay frosty,

John
