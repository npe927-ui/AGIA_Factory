The lesson starts with a fact that many people find shocking.

I have only ever had a tiny handful of clients who resisted changing what I wrote. 

They let my ads run uncensored in all their profitable glory, and I loved them for it.

All of us ended up making a lot of moolah together.

Then there are the worst client types. 

You know, the ones who, after consulting with their daughter, the English Lit major, or the boys down at the local watering hole…

… gleefully annihilate their own results, profit and reputations.

I know of no other industry where people have this audacity, by the way. 

Most people (rightly) wouldn't dream of interfering with their dentist.

"Hey! Let me drill on my molar - you’re doing it wrong! 

These same folks will routinely muck up and alter ad copy, no matter how accomplished the copywriter.

Then there are those clients who fall somewhere in the middle of these two extremes. 

They let you do your job...

... but with no small amount of fear and terror that you might write anything that could be construed as even slightly controversial. 

Dealing with clients is a skill you need to be a copywriter, especially as the world descends into mayhem and madness.

The secret is to quickly establish yourself as "the adult in the room."

But first, you have to realize what's happening.

You can't "fix" dumb…

... but you can (as a First Option) learn to identify who's gonna be trouble down the road. 

You can then choose not to engage with aggressively-idiotic or irrational players. 

(Ask them, for example, who gets to decide on final copy. Put it in your contract, even).

Clear communication with clients seems like it would be an obvious priority.

But crazy enough, it isn't.

If you're oblivious, chaos will consume you.
