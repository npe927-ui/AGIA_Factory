What your best customers need right now…Howdy.

I was once advising someone about "getting out into the marketplace as an expert"… 

… and the poor dude actually started shaking.

Just the thought of stepping onto the metaphorical stage of life, and performing sent him into a stuttering implosion.

This got me thinking about my own journey from stuttering fear-meister to swaggering bluster-bomb.

In business, my philosophy is: 

If you truly have a great product or service… 

… then shame on you if you don’t step forward and BE that top sales pro you need to be so your prospect can feel good about buying from you.

You can’t sell from your heels, people.

I am not an extrovert by any stretch of the imagination.

That’s why freelancing was a natural fit for me. 

It required long, lonely hours inside my head… 

… where I was excused from looking like the regular "suits" in the agencies because, as a writer, the more outrageous I appeared, the more they believed I must possess the "goods".

After around thirty gazillion private consultations, Hot Seats and meetings with clients… 

… all of whom tried to "alpha male" me into submission, because they wanted the writer (me) to be their slave…

… I started to think that maybe I had unwisely "defined" myself as an introvert.

The client needed copy and couldn’t produce it himself to save their lives. 

More than anything, they needed solid, actionable advice that I could deliver.

Thus, they were in no position to be dictating terms to me. 

That’s why I ain’t shy, professionally. Not anymore.

I’d figured out that it was NOT about me.

It was about the value of the content that I had to share.

My point:

If your market is crying out for someone to stand up and be the go-to-expert,  you really can (and should) step up. 

Like Keith Richards, you can get your chops honed to a degree that gives you enough confidence to be "onstage" (however you define the stage — it can be your website, an actual stage, emails, or face-to-face consultations).

Now you just have to deliver what the good folks paid to see.

Stay frosty,
