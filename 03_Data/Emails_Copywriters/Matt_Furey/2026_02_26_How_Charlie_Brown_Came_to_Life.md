# How Charlie Brown Came to Life

Ideas are free. Ideas are blowing in the wind.

They're coming to you 24 hours a day - but do you tune into them? Are you watching and listening to the idea channel that is all around you?

A cartoonist who is doing the illustrations for my latest book told me a story about Charles Schulz, the master creator of Charlie Brown, Snoopy, Peanuts, etc.

Schultz' four-strip cartoon panel ran for nearly 50 years. Ever wonder how he never ran out of ideas?

As it turns out, much of Schulz' day appeared to be nothing more than procrastination. What else would so many people call standing before a window and staring out of it?

But this was how Schulz relaxed his mind, how he entered the "Zen Zone." This is how he let ideas come to him.

To keep people away during this important time, whenever Schulz heard footsteps he would make himself appear busy drawing so that no one would interrupt. Then as soon as the intruders left he would go back to his window and let the ideas grow.

Hmmm. Ponder this one for a spell.

Here endeth the lesson!
