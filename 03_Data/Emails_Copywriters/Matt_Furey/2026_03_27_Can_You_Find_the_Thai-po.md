Can You Find the Thai-po in My Bio?

Yesterday I received an email from a guy who proclaimed that he had found a typo in my bio.

My first thought wasn't, "Oh no, what is it?"

It was, "I wonder if he gets it wrong."

The man wrote, "You wrote the following, 'In the fall of 1984, in order to help rebuild a doormat wrestling program...' it should read dormant not doormat."

It's amazing how often you can get struck in your lightening rod if you do a tiny tadpole's worth of research before you assume that a Type O isn't a typo.

If he had consulted Merriam, as in Webster, before emailing me he would have found the following:

doormat - noun - door·​mat ˈdȯr-ˌmat

https://clicks.aweber.com/y/ct/?l=osU7E1&m=3iAx2kvH03UFbNw&b=DHuJWoyphu63KwaA9QA75A

1. a mat placed beside or inside a door for wiping dirt from the shoes
2. one that submits without protest to abuse or indignities
3. a team that regularly finishes last
Now, the truth is that I am the master of the Thai-po. I dine on plates made from malapropism, and eat with a knife, fork and spoonerism.

Or as Sir Winston Churchill once told a grad student who felt justified in correcting the grammatical errors in the speech W.C. was to give, "Young man, you leave my speech uncorrected as is. This is the sort of criticism up with which I will not put."

Hear inneth the listen.
