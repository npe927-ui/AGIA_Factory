Going thru Hell

You may find this helpful.

It's a line that comes from Jerry Seinfeld, who said that a writer has to go through hell to get into heaven.

Heaven, in this case, being the finished script, book or product.

But do you really have to go thru hell?

That depends on your point of view. It depends upon how focused you are. It depends upon whether or not you can get yourself into your comfort zone.

When you're writing from outside your comfort zone, I suppose you're going through hell. When you're in your comfort zone, though, you have no concept of time, space, or even your "self." And you can surprise yourself with what comes through you.

This past Sunday, I got up later than usual, and started with the editing process for my latest, and what I've been told, my "greatest book."

I figured this round of editing would fly by pretty fast. And it did, but in a time-distorted, time-dilated way, where the long hours felt as though they were minutes.

11 hours of editing on Sunday, tweaking three chapters.

Followed by six hours yesterday.

Altogether, I reckon I've put 40 hours into this round of editing, which is more than I put into any of the previous rounds.

But why?

I don't have a good answer for that, other than to say, "Becuz."

All I know, or think I know, is that in the process of creating, you lose yourself - meaning you're not aware of yourself. You are only aware of the words you're playing with.

If you were able to observe the level of focus and concentration I had the past couple days, you might say, "Man, he really works hard."

I would argue that I'm not working at all, and I'm definitely not working "hard."

When you're doing what you want to be doing, when you're doing what you feel called to do, it's not work.

It's highly focused, deeply concentrated play.

This idea may be unappealing, perhaps even appalling  to you. But it is the way I see it.

I wonder, if being this focused is "going through hell," then what the hell is heaven?

Is there ever a "hell" in the process of writing and  a book?

There sure is. Hell is when you feel stuck. And guess what you're doing when you feel stuck?

Nothing.

But the second you begin writing, even when you have no idea what is going to end up being written, you are no longer in hell.
