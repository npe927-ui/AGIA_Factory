Q: Dear Matt,
How many books have you written and what percentage of them made you money?

Noah Manners

A: Dear Noah,
Interesting question because as it turns out I was asked the first part of this question yesterday while having lunch with three of my long-time students - all of whom have been with me for 20 years.

After I was asked this question I began running through the images of books I have written in my mind, as I named each title and gave it a number. This alone, for some reason, caused Jim, one of the men at the table, to say, "I'm impressed that you don’t even know the number."

What impressed him about that, I didn’t ask.

Anyway, as soon as I got to 16, I said, “There may be others that I’m not recalling at this time.”

And seconds later I heard myself saying, “Oh, there was also One Breath at a Time - so that makes 17 but again, there might be others I haven't thought of yet.”

A few minutes later I remembered two more books I had written, and a few hours later, another two. So by my best estimate, the current number is 21.

To answer part two of your question, none of my books "made money" as only the government and counterfeiters make money, but all of them collected money. Some collected a little bit, others collected a fair amount - some made me phat.

And the books that collected the most money were, to the best of my recollections, a surprise.

Now, to answer your unasked question, “Is writing a book the goal or is collecting money the goal?”

I’ve done it both ways and taught it both ways, but the best way is a suspenseful combination of forces, meaning that after you spend time writing the book, and time promoting it, something cosmic happens that leads to you getting your fair share - and quite possibly your “unfair share."

Some will lead you to believe that it's all about "the copy." And even though this is true - it's a half-truth.

There will always be other-than-conscious forces at play - which is why I avoid the hard work, over-thinking and over-analyzing trap. Writing is far easier, enjoyable and profitable when you give your subconscious mind all the hard work and heavy lifting while you relax and enjoy the ongoing show.

Divine Timing.
