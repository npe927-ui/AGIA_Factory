Writer's Limp

In sports there's a condition called the "Loser's Limp."

It's not an injury. It's not even a real limp.

It's a fake limp designed to avoid embarrassment and provide an excuse for not being able to win the game.

So instead of being "showed up," you put on a show.

This limp isn't something that only afflicts players at the pee-wee or teenage level, either. You'll see it with college athletes - as well as in the pros. Players who boast and brag about how they're this, that and the other thing - but then when another badass comes to town, "Ouch, I can't play. My hymnaroids are singing to me again."

Writers and creators can also give themselves an imaginary loser's limp. It can come in many forms and guises - all of which are some type of resistance to moving forward.

You can force yourself to plow through this resistance with will power - and this will work temporarily - or you can learn how to get into what I call the "Zen Zone" - that Zero Resistance state-of-mind that brings state-of-the-art results.

There are many tools in my "shed" for getting into this Zen Zone. These tools turn the hard work of writing into something playful and fun.

I use them daily. And I'm looking forward to teaching them to an army of writers who want to level up. Could you be one of them?
