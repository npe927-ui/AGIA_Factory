# The Hardest Writing in the World

**De:** Matt Furey <matt@mattfurey.com>
**Fecha:** Thu, 20 Nov 2025 17:04:03 +0000
**ID:** 19aa239217bcc0eb

Many people think that email writing is hard, hard work.

It can be.

But email writing is only hard if you're over-thinking, trying too hard, writing to impress, not letting it flow.

It's also hard when you don't know what causes people to become curious, to get emotional, and to want more.

Over 20 years ago I taught that email writing is similar to talk radio. I still agree with that statement. But if you're going to excel at talk radio, you better be entertaining, not just informing. Same goes for email.

Email takes study and practice - but much of it is getting out of your own way. Not trying to please your English teachers. Not trying to please anyone. Even yourself, much of the time.

Sounds oxymoronic, I know. But after almost 30 years of doing it, I'm pretty sure I know what I'm rapping about.

The truth is that getting out of your own way, being fast and loose, is just as important as knowing what to write and how to write it.

Part of the skill with email is learning about words and how they affect the brain. These are the triggers that cause people to keep reading.

The other part is learning to let your internal cannons fire, and doing so without angst, worry or fear.

In today's email subject line I used the word "hardest."

This word doesn't appear to be one that commands attention. After all, doesn't everyone want what is easiest?

So why use "hardest" instead of "easiest?"

Good question.

Stay tuned as there is much more to come.

And I'm pretty doggone sure you're going to luv it.
