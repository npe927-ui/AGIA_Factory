Ideas for Idi-ahts

When I was in sixth-grade, I thought Sister Synesia was an old, old lady who had to be at least 85.

Turns out she was around 50, and had many years to go. She lived to see the age of 94.

Sister Synesia didn't care too much for me. At that time the feeling was mutual, which compelled me to make  ventriloquistic noises during class. The noises never fooled her. She'd turn around and glare at me, then turn around again to grab a piece of chalk so she could write "M.F." inside a box on the upper right hand corner of the black board.

My initials inside this box got some chuckles and snickers - and not because they signified that I would be held in detention after everyone else went home.

I look back fondly on those detentions, which weren't limited to the ones Sister Synesia doled out. She was in good company as the other teachers didn't dig me either.

As fate might have it, I would now say that I learned more in detention at that school, St. Lawrence, than I did in class.

And that's because it was in detention that I met another nun, Sister Delores, who volunteered to help the other teachers who were at their whip's end. Sister Delores took me aside and began teaching me mind control.

She showed me how to start the day with 100 percent at the top of a sheet of paper, and every time "I caught myself" goofing off, I deducted five percent. The goal was to finish the day as close to 100 percent as I could and no one was keeping a tally but me.

This simple exercise worked wonders in my whirld, and it will work for anyone who uses it, albeit in a slightly different way.

You begin your day with an objective in mind, such as how many words you are going to write. Then you keep score.

No one else is watching you. It's you versus your objective.

Will you get it done? Will you find a way to put the number of words you had in mind on paper?

When you do, consider yourself a more-on.

You have more on the paper than when you started. This is far better than being an idiot, or an "idi-aht," as Sister Synesia pronounced it.

If you think I'm over-simplifying the writing process, I am, but for a good reason. It works.

If you want to create something everyday, begin with a target in mind.

Here endeth the lesson.
