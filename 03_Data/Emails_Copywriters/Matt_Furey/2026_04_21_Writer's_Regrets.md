Writer's Regrets

Q: Do you have any regrets as a writer?

A: One regret. Not writing more. What doesn't get written doesn't get published. What doesn't get published benefits  no one.

Q: But do you ever regret something you've written and published?

A: No.

Q: I understand but haven't you made errors and mistakes from time to time that you regretted?

A: Why would I regret a mistake? If I make an error I can write something the same day to correct it. Mistakes in writing are another thing. I make them every day. And in today's world of A.I., I believe I should be making a hole lotsa more of them.

Q: Why do you so that?

A: Because A.I. writing is inhumane. It's disgusting. It irks me. As soon as I spot it, which doesn't take long, I stop reading.

Q: But everyone is saying if you don't jump on A.I. you're going to get left behind. What is your response to that?

A: Who is "everyone?" I can assure you that there are plenty of people who share my opinion. And it won't be long before humans get sick of the inhumane and realize what they've done to themselves and to their children, and to societeee. When you stop using your brain, your brain deletes itself, without your permission I must add. It deletes the areas of the brain that are no longer being used. And guess what? You can't get them back once they've been deleted.

Here endeth the lesson.
