Great Writers Who Hate Writing

Q: Matt, I’ve heard the phrase, “Do what you love and the money will follow.” Do you think this is true? And is it possible to hate writing, be good at it and still make a lot of money?

A: Sure. Why not? Ya know, I don’t recall ever giving any thought to whether or not I love writing. I can say this, though, I love when I’m in the “Zero Resistance Zen Zone,” and I love when I write something that puts points up, something that knocks in the winning runs. Yes, I love that.

It’s more about doing what needs to be done to create something you want than it is about needing to love what you’re doing to create it.

Muhammad Ali said, “I hated every minute of training, but I said, “Don’t quit. Suffer now and live the rest of your life as a champion.“

I don't suffer when I'm writing because when I'm writing I have already put myself into the Zone - but I have suffered when I'm resisting doing the writing because I'm not in the Zone. And yes, that is painful.

The hardest part of writing is usually getting started, but it is only hard because we "make it hard" when it's really just as easy as getting into the shower. You can resist taking a shower, too, and some writers do. And I suppose that's why writers who don't break through their resistance and write, start to have stinking thinking about themselves.
