Don't Spank Your Monkey Mind

You have a monkey in your mind, and if you've practiced meditation, you may have attempted to silence it.

When this fails, what do most people do?

They spank and scold themselves, along with their monkey mind.

I do not condone such practices.

Instead of spanking your monkey mind, I believe in giving it a voice, giving it a lot of rope, giving it almost free reign, letting it out of its cage.

I let my monkey mind, my Curious George, meander.

I let the so-called Neanderthal man become Meanderthal Man - giving him access to more than "just ME."

You will be shocked and surprised at where your inner Meanderthal or Curious George will take you - if you'll let it speak.

Curious George may take you back in time (if you remember reading these books as a youth), or to the circus or zoo. He might even cook you some pancakes or take you to the beach.

Regardless of where this inner monkey takes you, you are wise to go along for the ride and record what you see and hear happening.

As I've stated before, writing begins with nothing but a word, a phrase or an idea that you write on a card.

After writing this word, phrase or idea, you can do what comedian Steven Wright does.

He records it, as he did for his novel, Harold.

Same thing I advise.

Steven Wright did not set out to write a novel, or even a short story.

All he did was write the word "Harold" on a sheet of paper.

Nothing after that, other than having a cup of coffee and pedaling a bicycle.

He then let his subconscious mind do the work. He let his brain stew on the word as he did something else.

When you let your subconscious do the work - it will. There's nothing woo-woo about this at all, once you realize that this is how you write when you're in a state of flow.

The ideas come to you of their own accord when you're not thinking.

And when the ideas come you flesh them out by adding flesh to the word you wrote. You give the word a body. You give it roots, limbs and leaves. Then you relax, breathe life into it and let it grow.

You do not force the words. You let the words grow within the infinite facility and faculty of your mind.

Doubting this process spanks the Curious George within, which is not only inhumane, but insane.

Free your monkey mind in your writing and your writing will set you free.
