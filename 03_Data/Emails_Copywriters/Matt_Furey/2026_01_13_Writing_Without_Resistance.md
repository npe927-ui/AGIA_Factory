Writing Without Resistance

Q: Matt, how do you get yourself to sit down and write instead of procrastinating on it?

A: Writers procrastinate for many reasons, but one of the biggest, if not the biggest reason, is the resistance they feel about the action that is required.

Here's a simple example: If your house is a mess and you want it to be clean, you attempt to calculate an answer to the question, How long is this going to take? And upon coming up with an answer, you say to yourself, "I don't have that much time right now." So, instead of doing something, you do nothing, other than find time to watch movies, tee-vee, talk on the phone, scroll on social media, and so on.

You put off any and all action because of the resistance you feel and your desire to avoid that feeling.

Ultimately, you can fight through this resistance and force yourself to "just do it" and that is what many writers would advise.

Not me.

Instead I look for and find a way to feel no resistance to writing?

Ask yourself: How much would you write if there was no resistance to taking action whatsoever? How many more emails, books, courses and so on would you create?

You can tell yourself that resistance is normal, that you have to battle it every day. Or you can do what I have done and figure out ways to get things done without a battle, without fighting through the resistance.

When you go after a writing goal without any resistance to taking action, the success you will enjoy is "off the charts." It's mind boggling.

Yesterday, I had an incredibly small writing goal. So small you would laugh. So small that it could be accomplished within 60 seconds.

But what was the result of focusing on that goal? That's the question.

The result was that I kept going - for another five hours, without any resistance, and I accomplished a much, much bigger objective than I thought I could.

What a great way to live.

You can fight writer's resistance if you want. You can engage in a daily battle with it and think there is no other way to go through life. Or you can follow a different approach, one that is so simple it's ridiculous.

The results of procrastination are ugly - but procrastination is rooted in avoiding the resistance you feel to action.

But when you remove resistance from the equation - there's no reason to avoid action.
