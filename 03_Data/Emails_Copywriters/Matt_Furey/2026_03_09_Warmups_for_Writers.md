Warmups for Writers

Q: Before you begin writing, do you do anything to warm up your brain, such as walking?

A: Yes, I do, but what I do each day varies based upon the type of writing I am doing, and how much.

For example, this morning I was a little groggy when I got up, which can be a good mental state to write from because there's less interference from your conscious mind. But today, the grog was an impediment. So I got up and practiced a series of deep breathing exercises mixed with movement, and by the time I was finished, which was around ten minutes, I was reeling and ready to go.

The key to the warmup is that you don't overdo it. If you exercise to exhaustion before creating, your brain may lack the energy it needs to do the job at an optimum level. So you'll need to observe what you do when you're at your best and make adjustments according how you perform.

For me the warmup is designed to rev up the brain but not to shorten or exhaust the circuits. More intense exercise is something I engage in when my creative work is finished.
