Small Daily Battles Win the Big War Over Words

Writing is WAR.

Or is it?

Writing can be a war, an ongoing fight to the finish. Or it can be a series of small winnable battles over blank sheets of paper, all of which build momentum and give you the victorious life you want.

Typically I don't write about the practice of writing as a military operation, as a battle, or even as a war. But sometimes there are no better metaphors.

If you observe all the wars over the course of inhumane history, you will clearly see that success is much more likely when the objectives are not overwhelming; but as soon as the goals are MASSIVE, no matter how close one side is to victory, something always happens to thwart the cause.

In fact, whenever the goal is HUGE, as in conquering the whole world - what happens?

Nothing but devastation.

Small battles are much easier to manage and win.

In a big battle, even when you have far greater technology, far greater resources, and a lot more money - all of it can be neutralized by an opponent who refuses to give up as you are lured into the swamps, the mountains, the caves and the deep freezing snow and ice.

The same is true when it comes to creating the life you want as a writer. If your objective is world conquest, world domination, you'll need much more than great skill, money and good luck.

But if you begin with small objectives that you can wrap your noggin around, and you focus on those objectives and bring them to a close, and do so over and over again, without even realizing it, your practice turns out victories that propel you upward.

This means you don't attempt to write an entire book at once. You write it one small chunk at a time.

Your small chunk could be four double-spaced pages of copy per day. It could be 10-20 pages of single-spaced copy. Or it could be a quotation, a paragraph, or a single page that is hand-written.

I have a book in the works that is hand-written on oversized postcards. Each day I write one exercise that people can use and greatly benefit from.

Another book in the works is much more extensive and time intensive. Ultimately, both books will see the light.

Neither of these books are akin to world wars. They are more of a daily game I'm playing around with myself to see what will eventually happen.

In many ways, I know the outcome of these daily doings, but ultimately the idea may run further than I imagined.

I know the foreseeable outcomes because I've done this so many times over the past 30+ years that there is a predictability to the method.

If I go for world-wide domination, if I make that the goal... if I attempt to conquer everything in one swelling swoop, nothing good will come from it.

A little bit at a time adds up to a TON when you are consistent and abide by the two R's: Relentless Repetition.
