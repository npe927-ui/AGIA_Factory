Scriptomania

If you have an intense love and desire to write, and it doesn't matter where you are or what you're doing because you will always have a way to record your thoughts, you could have scriptomania.

On the other side of the fence, if you have an obsessive fear of your writing being made public, and judged harshly, you're treading on ice that ain't so thick.

The out-of-control fear of your writing being judged is called scriptophobia. Yes, there is such a thing.

To an extent, all writers have had to overcome one fear or another, but it can be especially debilitating if you are writing for pay, or if you're writing with the idea in mind of making so much money that you could pay for all the tea in China, not to mention Japan, India and Korea.

Many people who are possessed with the knack for making comments on social media, and these people could be trolls, have no fear of writing and posting whatever idiotic ideas come to mind.

But these same people are stumped if they are sitting before a blank sheet of paper with the goal of writing an email or a book. They find themselves in a state of "DOI," of not having an inkling of what to write.

These people think they cannot come up with a single idea worth writing about. They feel blocked.
