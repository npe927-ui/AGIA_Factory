How to Write Nothing

Dear Matt,
I'm at my wit's end. Each day I try to come up with something to write about but in the end all I can come up with is a blank sheet. I feel that there is nothing to write about. I'm working hard to come up with ideas but nothing seems to work. You seem to have an endless supply of ideas to write about. What am I doing wrong? Or what should I be doing?

Shai Rider

Dear Shai,
Ah, I see the conundrum that's making your lack of writing humdrum. You're "trying" to write something from something, but this idea goes against the grain of how everything in the universe is created.

Having nothing to write about is something to write home about because all writing begins from nothing.

The blank page is a mirror of the world. It reflects the unlimited possibilities you can write about, all of which begin as not much of anything.

Choose something, anything, from the nothingness and begin writing about it and you will see how quickly nothing turns into something.

This is especially effective when you feel you have nothing to say. So say nothing and write SOMETHING.

I've been writing something from nothing each day for a long time because I see nothing as everything.

For example, I just finished my 96th Zen Mastery Newsletter, Writing and Drawing from Outside the Brain.

Before writing this newsletter, I came face to face with 16 blank pages. I had nothing to write about. I was plumply out of ideas. So I sat still and thought about nothing, and it wasn't long before nothing began to split into two somethings: writing and drawing.

Well, I knew next-to-nothing about drawing before I began this newsletter, but as I wrote about it, more material came to me from the immaterial, and it wasn't long before I had more threads to weave into the bulk of the material.

Here's what's amusing: After sending out the newsletter, people who are already really good artists told me, "Matt, what you wrote about is really something."

This made me laugh as the idea to write about drawing came from nothing. In fact, the subject of drawing wouldn't even qualify as the "last thing" I would ever consider writing about, and yet I did.

So embrace the blank page.

Get in touch with the "nothingness" and let it turn into something that surprises you.
