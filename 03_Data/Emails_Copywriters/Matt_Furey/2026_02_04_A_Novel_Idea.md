A Novel Idea

Dear Matt,
Have you ever written a novel, and if not, is that something you think you can do?

Hope Phule

Dear Hope,
I haven't written a novel yet - but I've come mightily close with The Unbeatable Man - a true story or memoir that reads the same as a novel. In fact, some of the names and a few of the details in it have been changed to protect the guilty.

When we break things down to the ridiculously simple, a novel is anything but novel. It's an old idea that keeps on circulating.

Going further, a novel is a short story that ends up being longer than it needs to be, so instead of making a long story short, you make a short story longer. Much, much longer.

The Unbeatable Man began as a short story that I gave as a Christmas present to family, clients and the few friends that any writer who takes himself too seriously can find.

If memory serves me incorrectly, it was about 20 pages. When the women who read it got to the end, they cried and told me so. As for the men, none cried who would admit it.

Being a man who is around 99% male, I will admit that I shed a few while writing it - and that's part of the key to great writing. It's not hard to shed tears when you write when you're emotionally into the story, regardless of length.

All novels have elements of truth in them, just as all true stories have exaggerations and hallucinations, intentionally or unintentionally. If you doubt me on this, just read any former President's autobiography and see if you can find a few kernels of truth.

So to answer your question succinctly, I already did.
