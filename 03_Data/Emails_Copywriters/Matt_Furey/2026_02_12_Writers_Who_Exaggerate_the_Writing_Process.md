Writers Who Exaggerate the Writing Process

Q: Dear Matt,
I've read and listened to a fair amount of writers talking about their process and most of them say they "worked hard." Now you're coming around and calling B.S. on them - saying its the opposite. Can you explain a little more thoroughly as I'm confused?
John

A:Dear John,
I feel your pangs. It is confusing. So let me paint a different picture for you.

When interviewed, most of these writers use cliches to explain their process. In doing so they regurgitate cute but myth-leading phrases such as:

"I just cut open a vein and bleed onto the page."

"I roll up my shirt sleeves and put in the work hard."

"You learn to write through blood, sweat and tears."

"You pull yourself up by your bootstraps and do the hard work."

Got any idea how many writers ever bled onto a page?

None.

How about the number who were sweating, or who sweated blood?

None.

And how many writers need to roll up their shirt sleeves or wear boots with straps?

ZERO!

Sure, I can picture the olden days, when a writer working in a newspaper office removed his suit and tie and rolled up his sleeves - but in today's world???

The truth is you can remove all your clothes and write naked at home, or at the beach. Even writers of yesteryear were not opposed to doing so. Some even wrote books while in prison. I'm sure that doing so was an 'escape' more than it was hard work.

In today's world you can even write books on your phone while sitting in a noisy coffee shop. I know at least two best-selling writers who did so.

I also know people who have jobs where they really do sweat. And I know people who have bled on the job. Some whom have even lost fingers - and worse. And yes, these workers wear boots and when they get too hot on the job, they might even roll up their shirt-sleeves.

But writers? Come on. Enough of the vaingloriousness. We are made different. We are CREATORS - not workers.

And MASTER CREATORS LIVE AND OPERATE IN THE ZEN ZONE!

Writing is only hard work when you make it so.

And the truth is you can make it far easier than ever when you know how to put your subconscious mind to work for you as you relax.

True, at times writing can be burdensome. There are moments when you cannot find the words to express yourself. But those moments are fewer and farther in between when you realize that YOU are the one making it harder on yourself than it needs to be. This is when you have an opportunity to "wise up" and stop doing it the wrong way.

And that, my friend, is when the magic happens. It's  the spaces in between the notes that make the music. It is not having your nose on the grindstone.
