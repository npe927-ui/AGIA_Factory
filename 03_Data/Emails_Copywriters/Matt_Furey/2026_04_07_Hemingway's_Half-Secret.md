Hemingway's Half-Secret

I'm not a fan of everything Ernest Hemingway wrote, but I am a huge fan of how he wrote The Old Man and the Sea.

And I will say that I am a full-fledged fan of any and all secrets Hemingway had about his method of writing.

And yes, he did have plenty of them.

One of them is a half-secret.

Hold on, lest you think this means something other than what it means. If you don't know for sure, then you don't know at all.

Here's the half-secret: Write half a sentence and leave the rest for later.

Laugh all you want, but there are valid reasons for doing such a thing, as one reason is that this activates the subconscious mind to "do the work," while you're not doing any work. Do you see how that works?
