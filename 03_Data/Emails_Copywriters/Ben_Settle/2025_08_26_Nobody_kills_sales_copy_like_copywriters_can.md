A true story: I once heard Email Players subscriber and the man Universally considered to be the World’s Greatest Living Copywriter Gary Bencivenga talk about one of his blockbuster control ads, that he had to convince his client to run. The reason? The headline. It was NOT about how to get rich at breakneck speed like all the other competing ads - many no doubt written based on incestuous swipe file culture - including the ones that “crushed” it in the market place. No, not Gary’s headline. His headline went the exact, polar opposite way. It not only toned down the hyped-up, high-energy headlines in money-related niches, but his headline told them how to get rich... slowly. Literally that was the headline. “Nobody wants to get rich slow” said his client. And then Gary talked about what he said to persuade his client to run it. (Not my tale to tell, you gotta hear it straight from him) Anyway, I got to thinking about that recently. And especially about how many of the best headlines ever written would probably be stamped "rejected!" today... and how the copywriters have to fight just to get them tested. I had a few clients like that myself in my client days. Guys sitting in their ivory towers. Only able to parrot what they heard at masterminds or on some marketing cruise. Nattering on about how a headline I wrote for their offer needed “benefits!” (some of my best headlines have had ZERO benefits in them) or it needed to be more like [insert whatever goo-roo they were listening to at the mastermind]... but nary a word spoken about what the actual MARKET wanted or needed to hear. Thank God they weren’t all like that. I actually had some really good clients. But here’s another example of what I mean: I don’t know if he had to fight his client on running it (I have never asked him), but the great A-list copywriter Doug D’Anna told me about a piece with a headline I know many clients today would balk at, think it’d get them laughed at by peers, maybe even secretly rewrite it themselves. The headline: “Can India Beat China?” Ooh. But THAT headline, at that moment in history, to that list... was what they cared about. Presumably they cared more about the answer to that question than the specific stock stories or tips or whatever he was selling was teaching. I suspect the list had their eyes glued to the newspapers each day seeking info on those two countries. Anyway, I don’t know where else I’m going with this. Except, maybe, to say this: I really believe a big reason for headlines getting progressively worse, and copywriters’ understanding of how to write them is getting progressively worse, is from two decades of social media click-bait headlines (ads, articles, posts, etc) addling brains about what works or doesn’t work. In other words, copywriters are thinking way too much like copywriters, and not nearly enough like their customers. It’s one of many reasons I never show my stuff to “copywriters.” Not on the A-list or the D-list. It’s not because I’m better than them, but it’s because they ain’t the market. Even Doug told me during our talk that’s why he doesn’t do critiques. As he put it: (paraphrased) “How can I help them if I don’t know the market?” Do what you want with that info.
