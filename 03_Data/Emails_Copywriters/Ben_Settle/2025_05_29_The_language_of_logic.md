One of the all-time great copywriting books is Eugene Schwartz’s classic: “Breakthrough Advertising” There are hundreds of tips and insights inside that book. And many of them are so un-sexy on the surface you will hardly ever see them discussed even though they can radically amp up sales, response, and engagement. One of those lessons is what Gene called: “The language of logic” Here’s what that means: You take your ad, email, sales letter, marketing piece... and “pepper” (not too much, but just enough...) words and language that have “built-in” credibility throughout your ad, but only wherever it makes sense and sounds natural. These words sound very logical and intelligent. And are often used in courts, scientific circles, legal arguments, etc. It’s like having 1000’s of years of linguistic credibility rubbing off on your advertising. Very powerful stuff. They can add fat layers of depth, nuance, fun, belief, drama and the list goes on. And while Schwartz talks about several of them in his book - which I highly recommend reading - if you want to see exactly how I used this concept in a direct mail campaign for our Low Stress Trading company see the June Email Players issue. I show you both test pieces. And, in the winner’s case, I analyze it word-for-word. Including showing you a couple parts where this “language of logic” was weaved in. A useful tool for the toolbox indeed. Especially in today’s ever more-jaded market places. In fact, that is the exact kind of customers we targeted: The super jaded, ultra skeptical, even completely broken when it comes to promises. And using the language of logic in just a couple spots, I believe, helped us cut through that. You can read more about this issue in the P.S.
