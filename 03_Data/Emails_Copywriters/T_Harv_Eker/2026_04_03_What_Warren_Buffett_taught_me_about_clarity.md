Hi Harv here.

Years ago, Warren Buffett, one of the wealthiest human beings who has ever lived, shared a thought experiment that changed the way
I think about everything.

He said: Imagine a genie appears when you're sixteen.

The genie says: "I'll give you any car you want. Brand new. Bow on top. It's yours tomorrow morning."

You're sixteen. You're losing your mind.

Then the genie says one more thing.

"One catch. This is the ONLY car you'll ever get. It has to last your entire life."

Now. How would you treat that car?

You'd read the manual five times. You'd garage it every night. You'd fix every scratch the same day it appeared.

You'd treat it like the irreplaceable thing it is.

Because it is.

So what was Warren's point? You only get one mind and one body. Most people treat them like a rental car they're returning
tomorrow.

But here's where I want to take this lesson… somewhere most people don't go.

The same principle applies to your financial life.

You only get one life. Are you driving it with clarity?
Or are you just... going along?

Let me tell you a quick truth from my life… For a long time, I couldn't get all the pieces working at the same time. Business
great, health awful. Health improved, business suffering. Money coming in, relationships falling apart.

Does this sound familiar?

I finally stopped. And I asked myself four very powerful questions. And those questions created something I wasn't expecting.

Clarity.

Here are the questions…

 * What do I REALLY want?

 * Why don't I already have it?

 * What would it actually feel like to have it?

 * How do I get there?

Simple questions. But most people never actually stop and ask them.

They just keep driving.

When I sat down and answered these honestly… for every area of my life, something shifted.

My health got better. My relationships improved. My business exploded. And I had more free time than I'd ever had in my life.

The vehicle didn't change.

The driver just got clear.

That is when it hit me that clarity leads to power. And power is the ability to act.

Without clarity, you're just busy. With clarity, you're unstoppable.

So here's something to get on today.

Before you hear from me again, take those four questions and write out your answers. All of them.
In every area of your life: money, health, relationships, freedom.

Don't rush question one. "What do I REALLY want?" is the hardest question most people have ever been asked.

But it's also the one that changes everything.

Make sense?
