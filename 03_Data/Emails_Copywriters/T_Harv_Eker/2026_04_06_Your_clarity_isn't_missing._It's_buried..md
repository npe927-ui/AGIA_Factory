Hi Nacho ,

Here's something most people have completely backwards.

They think clarity is something you FIND.

Like it's hiding somewhere out there. Some book, some course, some perfect moment that hands it to you on a plate.

But that's not how clarity works.

Clarity isn't found.  It's uncovered.

Let me tell you what I mean.

When I was young, I had a pretty clear sense of what I wanted out of life. Then I told my parents I was quitting school.

They lost it.
"You're gonna be a bum." 

"You're never going to amount to anything."

Now do you think that specific moment had an effect on me?

Of course it did.

And something similar happened to you. Maybe not the exact same words. But the same type of messages. About money. About what's
realistic. About who you're supposed to be and what you're allowed to want.

Parents. Teachers. Friends. The culture.

They installed a program in your head, before you were old enough to question it.

And that program is sitting right on top of your clarity.

Like fog sitting on water.

The clarity is there. It's always been there. It's completely natural to you.

But that fog… the old programming about money, about success, about what you deserve… it keeps you from seeing it.

When you remove the fog, you don't "find" clarity.

It was there the whole time.

And when you remove the fog, new opportunities appear. Not because the world changed.

Because YOU changed. Because when you're clear, you see things that were always right in front of you.

The path reveals itself.

So here's your practical exercise for today.

Think back on the messages you received growing up…
specifically about money and success.

What did your parents tell you, directly or indirectly, about wealthy people? About work? About what was possible for someone like
you?

Write them down. Even the ones that feel embarrassing or small.

Because the moment you can SEE the old program, you get to name it, look it square in the face, and it starts to lose its power
over you.

That's not therapy.

That's clarity work.

And it's the most important financial work you can do.

Not budgets. Not investment strategies. Not tactics.

The fog first. Then the path.

True or True?
