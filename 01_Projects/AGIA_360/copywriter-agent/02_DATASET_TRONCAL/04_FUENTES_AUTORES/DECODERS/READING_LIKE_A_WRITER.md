# Reading Like a Writer: The Precision Framework

## 1. The Iceberg Theory (Hemingway's Law)
- **Definition**: Only 1/8th of the story is above water (the prose). The 7/8ths below (emotion, history, subtext) provide the weight.
- **Rule**: If you know the truth, you can omit it, and the reader will feel it. If you don't know it, and omit it, the writing is hollow.
- **Application**: In copy, don't over-explain the "Why." Present the "What" so powerfully that the "Why" is felt.

## 2. Word Choice as Architecture
- **Rule**: Every word must have a structural purpose. If a word can be removed without the sentence collapsing, it is a "load-bearing error."
- **Application**: Micro-copy needs to be 100% load-bearing.

## 3. Sentence Rhythm
- **Rule**: Vary sentence length to control the reader's heartbeat. Short for impact. Long for immersion.
- **Application**: "Stop. Then explain. Then expand the horizon until they are ready for the next stop."
