# Stein on Writing: The Thriller Mechanics

## 1. The Crucible (The Locking Mechanism)
- **Definition**: A situation from which the characters cannot escape until the conflict is resolved.
- **Rule**: If a character can walk away, the tension is fake. The faster you "lock the door," the more authentic the drama.
- **Application**: In copy, create a "Locked Room" scenario (e.g., "The offer expires in 4 hours, and there's no other way to get this result").

## 2. Pacing through Omission
- **Rule**: Don't describe what is expected. Cut the "walking through doors" and "ordering coffee" unless it's a murder weapon.
- **Application**: In copy, remove the "Welcome to our newsletter" fluff. Jump straight to the "Crucible."

## 3. Character as Action
- **Rule**: Character is revealed through choices under pressure, not internal monologue.
- **Application**: Show the user's problem through their *failed attempts* to fix it, not just a list of pain points.

## 4. The Hook & The Cliffhanger
- **Rule**: Every chapter must end with a question or a change in status.
- **Application**: Every paragraph in a long-form sales letter must pull the reader into the next one.
