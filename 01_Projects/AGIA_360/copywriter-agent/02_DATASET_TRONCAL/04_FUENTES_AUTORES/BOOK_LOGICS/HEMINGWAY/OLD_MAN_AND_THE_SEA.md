# Hemingway: The Old Man and the Sea (Technical Logic)

## 1. Syntax of Survival
- **Pattern**: Polysyndeton (use of "and") to show the exhausting continuity of action.
- **Rule**: Connect actions with "and" to avoid hierarchical subordinates. Everything is happening *now*.
- **Application**: In copy, use "and" to stack benefits without pauses, creating a relentless flow of value.

## 2. Dignity through Detail
- **Rule**: Describe the *action* of the struggle, not the *feeling* of the struggle. The character's dignity is in their technical precision (e.g., handling the line).
- **Application**: Describe the user's workflow with technical reverence. Don't say it's "hard," show the grit of the specific process.

## 3. The Singular Focus
- **Rule**: One man, one boat, one fish. Extreme focus on a single objective per scene.
- **Application**: One message, one CTA, one clear result per landing page section.
